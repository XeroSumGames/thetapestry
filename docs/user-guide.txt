========================================
1. GETTING STARTED
========================================

Visit thetapestry.distemperverse.com to land on the World Map. You can browse
as a guest ("Ghost"), but to create characters or join campaigns you need an
account.

CREATING AN ACCOUNT
- Click "Create Account" in the left sidebar
- Enter a username, email, and password
- You start as a Survivor (player role)

SIGNING IN
- Click "Sign In" in the left sidebar
- Enter your email and password

THE SIDEBAR
The left sidebar is your main navigation. It contains:

  THE TAPESTRY
    Welcome to the Tapestry — introductory page
    The World — the interactive world map
    My Stories — your campaigns (GM and player)
    Rules — the full XSE SRD v1.1 reference (all 13 sections — §01 Overview
            through §08 Communities + Appendices A-D — public, no login required)

  SURVIVORS
    Creating a Survivor — tutorial page
    My Survivors — view and manage your characters
    Backstory Generation — full 10-step character creation
    Quick Character — fast 6-step build
    Random Character — instant auto-generated character

  BOTTOM
    Your username and role badge (Survivor or Thriver)
    Notification bell (shows unread count)
    Online survivors counter
    Log Out button


========================================
2. THE DASHBOARD & WORLD MAP
========================================

The main page shows the World Map — an interactive Leaflet map centered on
North America. You can:

- Pan by clicking and dragging
- Zoom with scroll wheel or +/- buttons
- Search for locations using the search bar (top right)
- Switch map styles: Satellite, Topo, Street, Voyager, Humanitarian,
  Positron, or Dark (buttons top right)

SIDEBAR TABS (right side, click "Pins" to toggle)

  PUBLIC — All approved world pins from all users. These are organized into
  folders by category. Click a folder to expand it. Click a pin to fly to it
  on the map and see its details, notes, and any uploaded images.

  MY PINS — Pins you created. You can edit, delete, or toggle them between
  public and private.

  CAMPAIGN — Revealed pins from campaigns you are a member of, grouped by
  campaign name. Click to fly to the pin location.

PIN CATEGORIES
Pins are grouped by type: Rumor, Location, Residence, Business, Church,
Government, Transport, Hospital, Military, Person, Danger, Resource, Medical,
Group, Animals, Community, Distemper Timeline, Settlement.

Each category has an emoji icon on the map. Rumor pins always show as a
question mark.

CREATING A PIN
Click anywhere on the map to place a pin. Fill in the title, notes, and
select one or more categories. You can attach images. Survivors' pins are
submitted for moderation. Thrivers' pins go live immediately.


========================================
3. CREATING A CHARACTER
========================================

There are three ways to create a character:

BACKSTORY GENERATION (/characters/new)
The full 10-step process following XSE rules:
  Step 0: Character Concept — name, age, gender, height, weight, three
          descriptive words, and a 1-2 sentence concept
  Step 1: Where They Grew Up — early life background, 1 Attribute CDP,
          2 Skill CDP
  Step 2: What They Learned — education, 1 Attribute CDP, 3 Skill CDP
  Step 3: What They Liked To Do — hobbies, 1 Attribute CDP, 3 Skill CDP
  Step 4: How They Made Money — profession, 2 Attribute CDP, 4 Skill CDP,
          vocational skills become available
  Step 5: What They Learned After — post-apocalypse, 3 Skill CDP
  Step 6: What Drives Them — pick or roll Complication and Motivation
  Step 7: Secondary Stats — auto-calculated from attributes
  Step 8: What They Have — primary weapon, secondary weapon, equipment
  Step 9: Final Review — print or save

Each step has a CDP budget. Spend points on attributes (RAPID) and skills.
Use the progress dots at the top to see which step you are on.

QUICK CHARACTER (/characters/quick)
A faster 6-step build with 5 Attribute CDP and 15 Skill CDP total:
  Step 0: Concept
  Step 1: Quick Build — spend all points at once, optional profession preset
  Step 2: Motivation & Complication
  Step 3: Secondary Stats
  Step 4: Equipment
  Step 5: Review

RANDOM CHARACTER (/characters/random)
Generates a complete character instantly — random name, stats, skills,
weapons, equipment, complication, and motivation. Saves automatically and
opens for editing.


========================================
4. MANAGING YOUR CHARACTERS
========================================

Go to My Survivors in the sidebar to see all your characters.

Each character card shows:
- Name, profession, three words, complication, motivation
- Portrait (click to enlarge)
- RAPID attributes (Reason, Acumen, Physicality, Influence, Dexterity)
- Skills grid
- Weapons (primary and secondary with ammo, condition, traits)
- Live trackers: WP, RP, Stress, Insight Dice, CDP, Morality

ACTIONS
- Edit — modify any field
- Print — printable character sheet
- Duplicate — copy the character
- Delete — permanently remove (with confirmation)

WEAPONS
Each character has two weapon slots (Primary and Secondary). Select from
Melee, Ranged, Explosive, and Heavy categories. Each weapon shows:
- Skill used (Melee Combat, Ranged Combat, Athletics, Demolitions)
- Damage formula
- RP percentage
- Range band
- Condition (Pristine +1, Used 0, Worn -1, Damaged -2, Broken = unusable)
- Ammo count and reload clips (ranged weapons)
- Weapon traits

HEALTH TRACKERS
- Wound Points (WP): Click circles to take/heal damage. At 0 = mortally
  wounded with death countdown.
- Resilience Points (RP): Click circles to take/heal. At 0 = unconscious.
- Stress: 5-segment bar. At 5 = stress check triggered automatically.
- Insight Dice: Earned on High Insight (double 6s) or Low Insight (double 1s).
  Can be spent to roll 3d6 or add +3 CMod.


========================================
5. CAMPAIGNS (STORIES)
========================================

Go to My Stories in the sidebar.

AS A GAME MASTER
- Click "New Story" to create a campaign
- Choose a setting (District Zero, Chased, Mongrels, or Custom)
- Name your campaign and add a description
- Your campaign gets an invite code — share it with players
- Edit: change name, description, map style, map center location
- Launch: opens the Table (the session play area)
- Share: copies the invite link to clipboard
- Delete: permanently removes the campaign

AS A PLAYER
- Get an invite code or link from your GM
- Go to the join URL or enter the code
- Select which character to play
- You appear in the GM's campaign

LEAVING A CAMPAIGN
- On your Stories page, click "Leave" on any campaign you are playing in


========================================
6. THE TABLE — PLAYING A SESSION
========================================

The Table is where gameplay happens. It is divided into sections:

HEADER BAR
From left to right:
- Campaign name and setting
- Start Session / End Session (GM only)
- Session number badge (when active)
- Tactical Map / Campaign Map toggle
- Share Map (GM, shares tactical map with players)
- Start Combat (GM, during active session)
- End Combat (GM)
- Checks dropdown (Perception, Gut Instinct, First Impression, Group,
  Opposed)
- Dashboard link
- Share (copy invite link)
- Overlay/Inline toggle (character sheet display mode)
- Restore (GM, heal wounded characters/NPCs)
- Sessions (view session history)
- Back / Exit

LEFT PANEL — LOGS / CHAT
Toggle between three views:
  Logs — chronological feed of all dice rolls, actions, and combat events
  Chat — in-character messaging
  Both — combined view

Type a message and click Send or press Enter to chat.

CENTER — THE MAP
Shows either the Campaign Map (with pins) or the Tactical Map (grid-based
combat map). Toggle with the header button.

RIGHT PANEL — GM TABS
The GM sees four tabs:
  NPCs — the NPC roster (create, edit, manage NPCs)
  Assets — campaign pins with drag-to-reorder
  GM Notes — private notes, drag-to-reorder, auto-saved
  Player Notes (for players) — submit notes to the GM

Players see:
  NPCs — revealed NPCs only
  Assets — revealed campaign pins
  Notes — their own session notes

STARTING A SESSION (GM)
Click "Start Session" in the header. The session counter increments and all
players are notified. During a session, dice rolls are logged and combat can
be started.

ENDING A SESSION (GM)
Click "End Session." A modal opens where you can write:
- Session Summary
- Next Session Notes
- Cliffhanger
These are saved to the session history.

CHARACTER SHEETS
Click a character in the left panel to view their sheet. In Inline mode it
appears in the center area. In Overlay mode it floats on top.

DICE ROLLING
During an active session, click any attribute, skill, or weapon on a
character sheet or NPC card to open the roll modal. The roll is:
  2d6 + Attribute Modifier + Skill Modifier + Conditional Modifier

OUTCOMES
  Low Insight (1+1) — Critical failure. +1 Insight Die. Weapon jam possible.
  Dire Failure (0-3) — Very bad result.
  Failure (4-8) — Standard failure.
  Success (9-13) — Standard success.
  Wild Success (14+) — Exceptional success.
  High Insight (6+6) — Critical success. +1 Insight Die.

INSIGHT DICE
Earned on Low or High Insight rolls. Can be spent before a roll:
- Roll 3d6 (keep best two) instead of 2d6
- Or add +3 to your Conditional Modifier
PCs only (NPCs only earn them if they are Antagonist type).

7. THE CAMPAIGN MAP
========================================

The Campaign Map shows an interactive world map with campaign-specific pins.

GM FEATURES
- Click the map to place a new pin
- Each pin has: name, notes, category, lat/lng
- Show/Hide pins to reveal them to players
- Drag pins to reposition
- Link pins to tactical map scenes (edit pin, select from Tactical Map
  dropdown)
- Promote pins to the world map

PLAYER FEATURES
- View revealed pins
- Click a pin to see its popup with name and notes
- Double-click a pin with a linked tactical map to open that scene

MAP LAYERS
Switch between: Street, Satellite, Dark, Positron, Voyager, Topo,
Humanitarian. Buttons appear in the top-right corner of the map.

SEARCHING
Type an address or place name in the search bar. Results appear as
suggestions. Click one to fly to that location.

PIN IMAGES
In the Assets tab (right panel), click a pin to expand it. If the pin has
uploaded images, they appear below the notes. Click an image to open it
full-size.


========================================
8. THE TACTICAL MAP
========================================

The Tactical Map is a grid-based canvas for combat positioning.

ACCESSING
Click "Tactical Map" in the header to switch from the Campaign Map. If no
scene exists, the Create Scene modal opens automatically. If scenes exist,
the most recent one activates.

GM CONTROLS (left panel)
- Scene dropdown — switch between scenes or create new ones
- Scene Name — editable text field
- Upload Map — upload a background image (JPG, PNG)
- Place Tokens — auto-place all initiative combatants on the grid
- Zoom +/- buttons and percentage display
- Grid controls:
    Grid ON/OFF toggle
    Grid color (White, Black, Grey)
    Grid opacity slider
- Cols/Rows — click +/- or double-click the number to type a value
- Cell (ft) — feet per grid cell (for distance calculations)
- Cell (px) — pixel size per cell
- Fit to Screen — reset zoom and scale so the map fills the view
- Lock/Unlock Map — lock prevents accidental resizing
- Show/Hide Ranges — color-coded range band overlay from selected token
- Delete Map — remove the background image
- Delete Scene — permanently remove the scene and all tokens

TOKENS
- Tokens represent characters and NPCs on the grid
- Each token shows: portrait or initials, name, WP bar, initiative badge
- GM can drag tokens to reposition (smooth drag, snaps to grid)
- Players can drag their own character's token
- Click a token to select it (shows info panel at bottom-left)
- Double-click a token to open its character sheet or NPC card
- Selected tokens show range circles (Engaged, Move, Weapon Range)

ZOOM & PAN
- Zoom slider in the top corner (25% to 400%)
- Hold Spacebar + click-drag to pan/scroll the map
- Cursor changes to a hand when panning

PINGING
- GM: double-click an empty cell to ping (orange pulse visible to all)
- Players: double-click to ping (green pulse)

MOVEMENT
During combat, click "Move" on the action bar. Valid cells highlight green.
Click a cell to move your token there.


========================================
9. COMBAT
========================================

STARTING COMBAT (GM only)
1. Click "Start Combat" in the header (requires active session)
2. Select which NPCs to include
3. Optionally choose a "Drop" character (acts first, alone)
4. All combatants roll initiative: 2d6 + ACU + DEX
5. Initiative order appears below the header, sorted highest to lowest
6. Tactical map auto-shares to players

THE INITIATIVE BAR
Shows all combatants in turn order with:
- Portrait, name, initiative roll
- Active indicator (highlighted)
- Round counter ("Round 1", "Round 2", etc.)
- GM can click X to remove a combatant or skip their turn

TURNS
Each combatant gets 2 actions per turn. The active combatant's action bar
appears below the initiative order. When both actions are used (or the GM
clicks Next Turn), play advances to the next combatant.

When all combatants have acted, a new round begins — everyone re-rolls
initiative and gets 2 fresh actions.

COMBAT ACTIONS (1 action unless noted)

  AIM — +2 CMod on your next attack. Must attack next or the bonus is lost.

  ATTACK — Roll your equipped weapon. Pick a target from the dropdown
  (filtered by range on the tactical map). Damage auto-applies on success.

  CHARGE (2 actions) — Pick a destination on the map (within 20ft), move
  there, then roll a melee/unarmed attack. If you have a ranged weapon,
  the charge uses an unarmed attack instead.

  COORDINATE — Roll Tactics to give allies within Close range (30ft) a +2
  CMod against a specific target.

  DEFEND — +2 defensive modifier against the next attack. Clears after
  being hit once.

  GRAPPLE — Opposed check. Pick an Engaged target (within 5ft). Both sides
  roll 2d6 + PHY + Unarmed Combat (defender can use Athletics if higher).
  - You win: target is Grappled (1 RP damage to target)
  - You lose: you take 1 RP damage
  - Tie: no effect, action consumed
  Grappled characters can only use "Break Free" (another opposed check).
  The grappler can only "Release" (free action).

  MOVE — Highlight cells within 10ft. Click to move your token.

  RAPID FIRE (2 actions, ranged only) — Two shots. First at -1 CMod,
  second at -3 CMod.

  READY WEAPON — Opens a modal with three options:
  - Switch: swap primary and secondary weapons
  - Reload: refill ammo from reloads (uses 1 reload clip)
  - Unjam/Repair: roll Tinkerer, Weaponsmith, or combat skill to fix
    a Damaged/Broken weapon

  REPOSITION — Free end-of-round positioning.

  SPRINT (2 actions) — Move 30ft (3x normal). Requires Athletics check.

  SUBDUE — Non-lethal attack. Full RP damage but only 50% WP damage.

  TAKE COVER — +2 defensive modifier for ALL attacks this round (not just
  the next one). Can only be used once per round. Enables "Fire from Cover"
  (2 actions: shoot while keeping cover defense).

  UNARMED — PHY + Unarmed Combat, 1d3 damage. Always available.

  STABILIZE — Appears when a nearby character (within 20ft) is mortally
  wounded. Roll Medicine to stop their death countdown. Must be Engaged
  (within 5ft) to actually perform it.

BLAST RADIUS (Grenades, RPGs, Molotovs)
When an explosive weapon hits, damage automatically applies to all tokens
near the blast center on the tactical map:
- Engaged (within 5ft): full damage
- Close (within 30ft): 50% damage
- Further (within 100ft): 25% damage
The attacker is excluded from splash damage. Results show in the roll notes.

MORTAL WOUNDS
When a character's WP reaches 0:
- Death countdown starts (4 + PHY rounds)
- If the character has Insight Dice, a modal appears asking whether to
  trade ALL Insight Dice to survive with 1 WP and 1 RP
- Only the PLAYER who owns the character can make this choice
- The GM sees a read-only version of the modal
- If not stabilized before the countdown expires, the character dies

ENDING COMBAT (GM)
Click "End Combat" in the header. Initiative order clears. All grapple
states are released. Mortally wounded PCs gain +1 Stress.


========================================
10. NPCs (GAME MASTER)
========================================

NPCs are managed in the right panel under the NPCs tab.

CREATING AN NPC
Click "+ Add NPC" to open the creation modal:
- Name
- Type: Friendly, Goon, Foe, or Antagonist (auto-sets base attributes)
- Portrait: choose from the bank (red=enemy, green=ally, white=neutral),
  upload an image, or pick from the portrait library
- RAPID attributes (manually adjustable)
- Skills (add from dropdown, set level)
- Weapon (primary, all categories available)
- Secondary Weapon (Foe and Antagonist only)
- Motivation and Complication (dropdown or random)
- Three personality words
- GM Notes (private, never shown to players)
- Status: Active, Dead, or Unknown

GENERATING AN NPC
Click "Generate NPC" and pick a type. The system auto-generates a random
NPC with appropriate stats, skills, weapons, personality, and a portrait
from the portrait bank.

CLONING AN NPC
Click the + button on the left side of any NPC card. Creates an exact copy
with an auto-numbered name (e.g., "Kincaid's Soldier #2"). The clone
appears directly below the original.

NPC CARD ACTIONS
Each NPC in the roster shows:
- Drag handle (reorder by dragging)
- Delete button (X, with confirmation)
- Clone button (+)
- Name (wraps to two lines if long)
- Status badge (Active/Dead/Unknown)
- Show/Hide button (reveal to players)
- Fight button (add to combat initiative, during combat only)
- Map button (place/remove token on tactical map)
- Portrait

Click an NPC to open its full card with:
- RAPID attributes (clickable for rolls during session)
- WP/RP dot trackers
- Skills (clickable for rolls)
- Unarmed attack button
- Weapon attack button
- Equipment attacks
- Stabilize button (if mortally wounded)
- Restore button (if dead, resets to full HP)
- Edit, Close buttons

REVEALING NPCs TO PLAYERS
Click "Show" on an NPC card to reveal them to players. Players will see the
NPC's name, portrait, and type. Click "Hide" to remove visibility.
Use "Show All" / "Hide All" in the header to toggle all at once.

PUBLISHING TO WORLD
From the NPC edit modal, click "Publish to World Library" to submit the NPC
for inclusion in the shared NPC library. Other GMs can import published NPCs
into their campaigns.


========================================
11. PINS & THE WORLD MAP
========================================

CAMPAIGN PINS (Assets tab on the Table)
- Click the map to place a pin at that location
- Each pin has: name, notes, category, coordinates
- GM can show/hide pins to control player visibility
- Drag pins to reorder in the sidebar
- Link a pin to a tactical map scene (in edit mode, select from the
  Tactical Map dropdown). Linked pins show a map icon. Double-click to
  open that tactical scene.
- Pin images: click a pin in the sidebar to expand it and view uploaded
  images

WORLD MAP PINS
The world map (/map or via "The World" in sidebar) shows all public pins.

Creating a pin:
1. Click anywhere on the map
2. Enter title, notes
3. Select categories
4. Optionally attach images
5. Save

Survivors' pins are submitted as Rumors (pending moderation).
Thrivers' pins go live immediately as GM pins.

Pin visibility on the map can be toggled per-folder using the eye icon
next to each category folder in the sidebar.


========================================
12. NOTIFICATIONS
========================================

Click the bell icon next to your username to see notifications.

NOTIFICATION TYPES
- New Player — someone joined your campaign
- Player Left — someone left your campaign
- Session Opened — your GM started a session
- New Survivor — someone created an account (Thriver only)
- Pin Submitted — a pin needs moderation (Thriver only)
- Pin Approved/Rejected — your submitted pin was reviewed

ACTIONS
- Click a notification to navigate to it
- Click X to delete a single notification
- "Mark all read" clears unread indicators
- "Delete all" removes all notifications


========================================
13. ROLES: SURVIVORS & THRIVERS
========================================

SURVIVOR
The default role for all players. Survivors can:
- Create and manage characters
- Join campaigns
- Play at the Table
- Submit pins as Rumors (pending moderation)
- View the world map and public pins

THRIVER
An elevated role granted by administrators. Thrivers get everything
Survivors have, plus:
- Moderation Queue — approve or reject submitted pins
- Pins go live immediately (no moderation wait)
- Portrait Resizer tool
- System Logs access
- Copy Map Position tool
- User management in /moderate (view emails, change roles, suspend/delete)
- Toggle pins between Public and Private directly
