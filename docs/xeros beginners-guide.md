&#x20;2. PINS, NOTIFICATIONS, AND ROLES (GM AND PLAYER)

====================================================================



PINS

\----

A pin is a labeled point on a map. Pins exist in two places:



1. &#x20; WORLD-MAP PINS — placed on the global world map (Chapter 3).
2. &#x20; Visible to everyone (eventually — see "Moderation" below).



CAMPAIGN-MAP PINS are placed inside a Story by the GM and are only visible to that Story's players, and only when the GM has shown the pin. Pins are hidden by default.



Every pin has:

&#x20; - Title and notes

&#x20; - One or more categories (changes the icon on the map)

&#x20; - A latitude / longitude

&#x20; - Optional uploaded images



Pin categories — and their map icons:



&#x20;   Rumor (❓)         Location           Residence Business           Church             Government Transport          Hospital           Military Person             Danger             Resource Medical            Group              Animals Community          Distemper Timeline Settlement



The "Rumor" category is the special one — see "Moderation" below and Chapter 12.



\[screenshot: world map with various pin icons]





MODERATION (WORLD-MAP PINS ONLY)

\--------------------------------

When a Survivor places a pin on the world map, it goes into a moderation queue and shows on the map as a Rumor (❓) until a Thriver reviews it. After review, the Thriver either approves it and it goes live with whatever category the submitter chose, or rejects it and it goes back to the author.



Thriver-placed pins skip moderation and go live immediately.

Campaign-map pins skip moderation entirely — they're private to the Story.



NOTIFICATIONS

\-------------

Click the bell icon in the user panel to open your notification list.



You'll get a notification for things like:

&#x20; - New Player joined your Story

&#x20; - Player Left your Story

&#x20; - Session Opened (your GM started a session)

&#x20; - Module Updated (a Rumor you've subscribed to has a new version — see Chapter 12)

&#x20; - Pin Approved or Rejected (your world-map pin was reviewed)

&#x20; - New Survivor signed up (Thrivers only)

&#x20; - Pin Submitted, awaiting moderation (Thrivers only)



Click any notification to jump to it. Use "Mark all read" to clear the red dot, or "Delete all" to wipe the list.



ROLES — INSIDE A STORY

\----------------------

Every Story has exactly one GAME MASTER (GM) and any number of PLAYERS:



&#x20; GAME MASTER (GM) — the person who created the Story. The GM:

&#x20;   - Creates and edits all NPCs, pins, tactical scenes, and handouts.

&#x20;   - Starts and ends sessions.

&#x20;   - Starts and ends combat.

&#x20;   - Decides what's revealed to players (NPCs and pins are hidden by default).

&#x20;   - Has full edit access to everything in the Story.

&#x20;   - Can launch the world map for other players.



&#x20; PLAYER — anyone who has joined the Story. A player:

&#x20;   - Brings ONE of their characters to the table.

&#x20;   - Rolls dice for that character.

&#x20;   - Sees only revealed NPCs and pins.

&#x20;   - Can place tokens for their own character on the tactical map (but not anyone else's).

&#x20;   - Can submit notes to the GM.



A player can be a GM in another Story at the same time — your role is per-Story, not per-account.

&#x20;3. THE WORLD MAP

====================================================================



Two maps exist in The Tapestry: the global WORLD MAP, and each Story's CAMPAIGN MAP. They use the same engine and most of the same controls.





THE WORLD MAP — /map

\--------------------

Click "The World" in the sidebar. You'll see an interactive map centered on North America — that's the world of Distemper.



CONTROLS

&#x20; - Pan: click and drag.

&#x20; - Zoom: scroll wheel, or the +/− buttons.

&#x20; - Search: top-right — type a place or address, click a suggestion to fly there.

&#x20; - Map style: top-right — Satellite, Topo, Street, Voyager, Humanitarian, Positron, or Dark.



\[screenshot: map with style toggle highlighted]



PINS PANEL (right side — toggle with "Pins")

&#x20; PUBLIC tab — every approved world pin, organized into folders by category. Click a folder to expand. Click a pin to fly to it on the map and see its notes and images.



&#x20; MY PINS tab — pins you placed. Edit, delete, or toggle public/private.



&#x20; CAMPAIGN tab — pins from any Story you're in that the GM has revealed.



The eye icon next to each category folder hides or shows that whole category on the map.



PLACING A PIN

&#x20; Click anywhere on the empty map. A form opens — fill in the title, notes, and one or more categories. Optionally upload images. Save.



&#x20; As a Survivor, your pin goes to the moderation queue and shows as a Rumor (❓) until a Thriver reviews it (see Chapter 2). As a Thriver, it goes live immediately.





THE CAMPAIGN MAP

\----------------

Each Story has its own map, accessible from the Table (Chapter 6). The campaign map has the same controls as the world map, but with a few extra GM tools:



&#x20; - Click the empty map to place a campaign pin (instant — no moderation).

&#x20; - Drag pins to reposition.

&#x20; - Show / Hide a pin to control whether players see it.

&#x20; - Link a pin to a tactical scene — players can then double- click the pin to open that scene (Chapter 7).

&#x20; - Promote a pin to the world map (sends it to moderation as a Rumor for global visibility).



Players see only revealed pins. Click any pin to read its notes and view its images.



Map style and search work the same as on the world map. The GM picks the default map style and starting location when creating the Story.

&#x20;4. CREATING A CHARACTER

====================================================================



In Distemper a character is called a SURVIVOR. The Tapestry has four ways to make one. They're all listed under "Survivors" in the sidebar.



Pick the one that matches how you want to start:



&#x20; BACKSTORY GENERATION   slowest, most authored — \~30 min.

&#x20; PARADIGMS              pick a 1-click template, then tune.

&#x20; QUICK CHARACTER        speed-build, \~5 min.

&#x20; RANDOM CHARACTER       1 click. Done.



All four produce the same kind of character. They differ only in how much you author up front.





WHAT'S IN A CHARACTER

\---------------------

Before you start, a few terms:



&#x20; RAPID — five core attributes: Reason, Acumen, Physicality, Influence, Dexterity. Range −2 to +5 (newcomers usually end up with values from 0 to +3). The SRD has the full descriptions.



&#x20; SKILLS — trained abilities like Melee Combat, Medicine, Tactics, etc. Each skill has a level 0-4. The SRD lists all skills.



&#x20; CDP (Character Development Points) — your build budget. You spend CDP to raise RAPID attributes and skills.



&#x20; MOTIVATION and COMPLICATION — narrative anchors. Why your character keeps going, and what's broken about them. You can pick from a list or roll randomly.



&#x20; WP (Wound Points) and RP (Resilience Points) — the two health tracks. WP is hard physical damage; RP is fatigue, scrapes, bruises. WP at 0 = mortally wounded. RP at 0 = unconscious.



&#x20; STRESS — a 5-pip track. Pip 5 forces a Stress check. Stress is added when bad things happen — including hitting WP=0 or

&#x20; RP=0.



&#x20; INSIGHT DICE — earned on extreme dice rolls (double 1s or double 6s). Spend one to roll 3d6 (keep best 2) instead of 2d6, OR add +3 to your roll's situational modifier.



&#x20; WEAPONS — every character has a Primary and Secondary slot (one melee, one ranged is the usual pairing). Each weapon has damage, range, ammo (if ranged), and a condition track that affects accuracy.





BACKSTORY GENERATION — /characters/new

\--------------------------------------

The full 10-step authored wizard. Each step has a CDP budget you spend before moving on.



&#x20; Step 0 — Character Concept. Name, age, gender, height, weight, three descriptive words, and a 1-2 sentence concept.

&#x20; Step 1 — Where They Grew Up. Early life. 1 Attribute CDP and 2 Skill CDP.

&#x20; Step 2 — What They Learned. Education. 1 Attribute CDP and 3 Skill CDP.

&#x20; Step 3 — What They Liked To Do. Hobbies. 1 Attribute CDP and 3 Skill CDP.

&#x20; Step 4 — How They Made Money. Profession. 2 Attribute CDP and 4 Skill CDP. Vocational skills unlock here.

&#x20; Step 5 — What They Learned After. Post-apocalypse skills.

&#x20;          3 Skill CDP.

&#x20; Step 6 — What Drives Them. Pick or roll a Motivation and a Complication.

&#x20; Step 7 — Secondary Stats. Auto-calculated. WP, RP, Insight Die count, Movement, etc.

&#x20; Step 8 — What They Have. Primary weapon, secondary weapon, one piece of equipment, one incidental.

&#x20; Step 9 — Final Review. Save, print, or rename.



The progress dots at the top of the wizard tell you where you are. You can go back at any time.



\[screenshot: backstory wizard mid-flight, progress dots visible]





PARADIGMS — /characters/paradigms

\---------------------------------

A Paradigm is an archetype — a pre-built RAPID + skill loadout the SRD ships with (12 of them, e.g. "Soldier", "Doctor", "Mechanic"). Pick one and the system creates a character seeded with that template, plus a rolled Motivation and Complication.



You land on the FINAL REVIEW step where you can rebalance attributes and skills before saving — within the standard creation caps (no attribute or skill above +3 at character creation).



\[screenshot: Paradigms picker grid]





QUICK CHARACTER — /characters/quick

\-----------------------------------

A 6-step build with one combined CDP pool: 5 Attribute CDP and 15 Skill CDP. Spend everything in one place.



&#x20; Step 0 — Concept.

&#x20; Step 1 — Quick Build (spend all CDP at once; optional profession preset).

&#x20; Step 2 — Motivation and Complication.

&#x20; Step 3 — Secondary Stats (auto).

&#x20; Step 4 — Equipment.

&#x20; Step 5 — Review.





RANDOM CHARACTER — /characters/random

\-------------------------------------

One click — the system rolls a complete character: name, attributes, skills, weapons, equipment, motivation, complication, and a portrait. It saves automatically and opens the edit page in case you want to change anything.





MANAGING YOUR CHARACTERS — /characters

\--------------------------------------

"My Survivors" in the sidebar. Each card shows:



&#x20; - Name, profession, the three descriptive words

&#x20; - Motivation and Complication

&#x20; - Portrait (click to enlarge)

&#x20; - RAPID attributes

&#x20; - Skills grid

&#x20; - Weapons (primary and secondary, with traits and ammo)

&#x20; - Live trackers: WP, RP, Stress, Insight Dice, CDP, Morality



ACTIONS

&#x20; Edit       — change any field Print      — printable character sheet Duplicate  — copy the character Delete     — permanent (asks first)





HEALTH TRACKERS

\---------------

Click the WP and RP circles to take or heal damage. Click Stress pips the same way. Insight Dice are earned automatically by your rolls; you spend them from the roll modal at the table.



Reaching 0 WP starts a death countdown (4 + Physicality rounds); a nearby ally has to roll Medicine to stabilize you (Chapter 8).

If you have any Insight Dice, the system will ask whether you want to spend them ALL to survive with 1 WP and 1 RP — that's a one-way ticket; only you can make that choice.



Reaching 0 RP makes you unconscious. Any time you reach 0 in either track, you also gain 1 Stress.





WEAPONS

\-------

Each weapon has:

&#x20; - Skill used (Melee Combat, Ranged Combat, Athletics for explosives, Demolitions for placed charges)

&#x20; - Damage formula

&#x20; - RP percentage (how much of the damage is RP vs WP)

&#x20; - Range band (Engaged 5ft, Close 30ft, Mid 100ft, Far 200ft+)

&#x20; - Condition: Pristine +1, Used 0, Worn −1, Damaged −2, Broken (unusable)

&#x20; - Ammo and reload clips (ranged weapons only)

&#x20; - Weapon traits (e.g. Burst, Two-Handed, Loud)

&#x20;5. CREATING A STORY

====================================================================



A STORY is your campaign — the whole ongoing thing. Inside one Story you can run encounters (single fights or scenes), adventures (multi-session arcs), or full campaigns (long-running storylines spanning many sessions). The Tapestry doesn't enforce a hierarchy here — the Story is the only formal container, and you organize the content inside it however you like.





CREATING ONE — /stories/new (GM)

\---------------------------------

Click "My Stories" in the sidebar, then "+ New Story." Fill in:



&#x20; - Name (required).

&#x20; - Description (optional).

&#x20; - Setting — pick one:

&#x20;     Custom         — start blank.

&#x20;     District Zero  — pre-loaded with the District Zero sourcebook NPCs, locations, and pins.

&#x20;     Chased         — Delaware setting, the "Chased" magazine.

&#x20;     Mongrels       — the canonical demo campaign.

&#x20; - Map style and starting location (the campaign map's default view).

&#x20; - OR — start from a Rumor (a published module). See Chapter 12.



You become this Story's GM automatically.



When created, the Story gets an INVITE CODE / LINK. Share that with your players — see "Joining" below.





THE STORY PAGE — /stories/<id>

\------------------------------

Lists everything about your Story:



&#x20; Launch        — opens the Table (Chapter 6).

&#x20; Edit          — change name, description, map style.

&#x20; Clone         — copy the entire Story as a new one.

&#x20; Share         — copies the invite link to your clipboard.

&#x20; GM Kit        — printable summary (legacy; being replaced by Rumors — Chapter 12).

&#x20; Sessions      — full session history.

&#x20; Snapshots     — saved states of the campaign you can roll back to.

&#x20; Modules       — the Rumors / module subscription panel (Chapter 12).

&#x20; 📦 Publish    — publish this Story's content as a Rumor Module       (Chapter 12).

&#x20; Delete        — permanent (asks first).



\[screenshot: story landing page with action buttons]





JOINING A STORY (PLAYER)

\-------------------------

The GM gives you an invite code or link. Two ways to use it:



&#x20; - Click the link directly. The "Join" page opens. Pick which of your characters to bring (Chapter 4). Confirm.

&#x20; - Or — go to /stories/join and paste the code.



You appear on the GM's player roster immediately and a notification fires for the GM.





LEAVING

\-------

On your "My Stories" page, click "Leave" on any Story you're a player in. (GMs can't leave their own Story; they delete it instead.)





ENCOUNTERS, ADVENTURES, AND CAMPAIGNS — INFORMAL

\-------------------------------------------------

There's no "+ New Encounter" or "+ New Adventure" button. The Tapestry's content tools — NPCs, pins, tactical scenes, handouts

— are flat inside a Story. Your encounters and adventures are just groupings YOU keep track of. A typical pattern:



&#x20; - One TACTICAL SCENE for an encounter (the battle map + grid).

&#x20; - Some NPCs to populate it.

&#x20; - A campaign-map PIN where the encounter happens.

&#x20; - A HANDOUT or two with details.



When you build a multi-session arc, you just stack more of the same. When you finish a Story, you can publish ALL the content as a Rumor (Chapter 12) so other GMs can run it.

&#x20;6. THE TABLE AND SESSIONS

====================================================================



The TABLE is where actual play happens. Click "Launch" on your Story page (or "Open the Table" from the player join screen) to get there.



The Table fills the whole screen — no sidebar.





THE HEADER BAR

\--------------

Across the top of the Table, left to right:



&#x20; Campaign name and setting badge Start Session / End Session   (GM) Session number badge          (when active) Tactical Map / Campaign Map toggle Share Map                     (GM — pushes the tactical map to the players) Start Combat / End Combat     (GM, requires active session) Checks ▾                      (Perception, Gut Instinct, First Impression, Group, Opposed, Recruit) Community ▾                   (Chapter 9) Dashboard link Share                         (copy invite link) Overlay / Inline toggle       (character sheet display mode) Restore                       (GM — heal everyone) Sessions                      (history) Back / Exit





THE LEFT PANEL — LOGS AND CHAT

\------------------------------

Three views, toggled at the top of the panel:



&#x20; LOGS  — every dice roll, action, and combat event in chronological order. Color-coded.

&#x20; CHAT  — in-character messaging.

&#x20; BOTH  — combined.



Type a message and press Enter to send.





THE CENTER — THE MAP

\--------------------

Either the Campaign Map (Leaflet, with pins — Chapter 3) or the Tactical Map (the grid for combat — Chapter 7). Toggle between them with the header button. Players see whatever the GM has shared.



Click a character or NPC token (or their entry in the left panel) to open their character sheet. INLINE mode renders the sheet in the center; OVERLAY mode floats it on top.



\[screenshot: Table view with header, logs panel, map, GM tabs]





THE RIGHT PANEL — GM / PLAYER TABS

\----------------------------------

The GM sees:

&#x20; NPCs          — full NPC roster (Chapter 10).

&#x20; Assets        — campaign pins, drag to reorder.

&#x20; GM Notes      — private notes, auto-saved.

&#x20; Player Notes  — submissions from players.



Players see:

&#x20; NPCs          — only revealed NPCs.

&#x20; Assets        — only revealed pins.

&#x20; Notes         — your own session notes.





STARTING A SESSION (GM)

\-----------------------

Click "Start Session" in the header. The session counter increments and every player in the Story is notified (Chapter 2). Until a session is active you can't roll dice or start combat.





DICE ROLLING

\------------

With a session active, click ANY attribute, skill, or weapon on a character sheet or NPC card to open the roll modal. The formula is:



&#x20;   2d6 + Attribute Modifier (AMod) + Skill Modifier     (SMod) + Conditional Mod    (CMod — situational)



Pick a CMod modifier (0 by default; the modal lists relevant ones based on your situation). Optionally spend an Insight Die (see "Insight Dice" below). Click Roll.



OUTCOMES (the standard XSE ladder): 1+1    Low Insight       Critical failure. +1 Insight Die.

&#x20;                            Weapon jams possible.

&#x20;   0–3    Dire Failure      Very bad result.

&#x20;   4–8    Failure           Standard miss.

&#x20;   9–13   Success            Standard hit.

&#x20;   14+    Wild Success      Exceptional success.

&#x20;   6+6    High Insight      Critical success. +1 Insight Die.



Both Low Insight (1+1) and High Insight (6+6) are matched-dice events — they grant an Insight Die in addition to the outcome.





INSIGHT DICE

\------------

Earned on Low or High Insight rolls. Stored on your character.

PCs only — NPCs only have them if they're the Antagonist type.



Spend an Insight Die before any roll for ONE of:

&#x20; - Roll 3d6 (keep best two) instead of 2d6.

&#x20; - Add +3 to your CMod.



You can also spend ALL of your Insight Dice at once to survive a mortal wound (Chapter 8).





ENDING A SESSION (GM)

\---------------------

Click "End Session." A modal opens:



&#x20; Session Summary       — what happened this session.

&#x20; Next Session Notes    — what's queued for next time.

&#x20; Cliffhanger           — the one-line tease.



These get stored in your Story's Sessions history.





SESSION HISTORY

\---------------

Click "Sessions" in the header (or visit /stories/<id>/sessions) to see every session you've run, with summaries, cliffhangers, and roll counts.

&#x20;7. THE TACTICAL MAP

====================================================================



The Tactical Map is a grid-based canvas for combat positioning.

You access it from the Table — click "Tactical Map" in the header to swap from the Campaign Map.



If your Story has no scenes yet, the Create Scene modal opens automatically. If scenes exist, the most recent one activates.



Each scene has:

&#x20; - A name and an optional background image.

&#x20; - A grid (rows, columns, cell size in feet AND in pixels).

&#x20; - Tokens (PCs, NPCs, and object tokens).



\[screenshot: tactical map with grid + tokens, GM controls visible]





GM CONTROLS (LEFT PANEL)

\------------------------

&#x20; Scene dropdown      — switch scenes or create a new one.

&#x20; Scene Name          — editable.

&#x20; Upload Map          — upload a JPG or PNG background.

&#x20; Place Tokens        — auto-place every initiative combatant on the grid.

&#x20; Zoom +/-, %         — zoom in / out / reset.

&#x20; Grid ON/OFF         — toggle the grid overlay.

&#x20; Grid color          — White, Black, or Grey.

&#x20; Grid opacity        — slider.

&#x20; Cols / Rows         — +/- buttons or double-click to type.

&#x20; Cell (ft)           — feet per cell (drives range bands).

&#x20; Cell (px)           — pixels per cell (default 35; visual).

&#x20; Fit to Screen       — reset zoom and pan.

&#x20; Lock / Unlock Map   — prevents accidental resizing.

&#x20; Show / Hide Ranges  — color-coded range overlay around the selected token.

&#x20; Delete Map          — remove the background.

&#x20; Delete Scene        — permanent.





TOKENS

\------

Every token shows portrait or initials, name, a WP bar, and (in combat) an initiative badge. Token behavior:



&#x20; - GM can drag any token. Players can drag only their own character's token.

&#x20; - Click a token to select it. Selected tokens show range circles for Engaged, Move, and Weapon Range.

&#x20; - Double-click a token to open its character sheet (PC) or

&#x20;   NPC card.

&#x20; - Right-click (or long-press on touch) a token for contextual actions during combat.





ZOOM, PAN, AND PINGING

\----------------------

&#x20; Zoom slider:        25% to 400%.

&#x20; Spacebar + drag:    pan the view (the cursor turns into a hand).

&#x20; Double-click empty cell: ping. GM = orange pulse. Player = green pulse. Visible to everyone watching.





SHARING THE TACTICAL MAP

\------------------------

"Share Map" in the Table header pushes the current tactical map to all players in the Story. Combat starts share it automatically.





====================================================================

&#x20;8. COMBAT

====================================================================



Combat is a structured mode you enter from the Table during an active session. The GM starts and ends it.





STARTING COMBAT (GM)

\--------------------

1\. Click "Start Combat" in the Table header.

2\. Pick which NPCs are in this fight.

3\. Optionally choose a "Drop" character — they act first, alone, before the rest of the round.

4\. Every combatant rolls initiative: 2d6 + Acumen + Dexterity.

5\. The initiative bar appears below the header, sorted highest to lowest.

6\. The tactical map auto-shares to players.



\[screenshot: initiative bar with portraits + numbers]





THE INITIATIVE BAR

\------------------

Shows everyone in turn order: portrait, name, initiative roll, and an active highlight on whoever's turn it is. The round counter ("Round 1", "Round 2"...) sits next to the bar.



The GM can click X on any combatant to remove them or skip their turn.





TURNS AND ROUNDS

\----------------

Every combatant gets 2 ACTIONS per turn. The active combatant's action bar appears below the initiative order. When both actions are spent (or the GM clicks Next Turn), play moves to the next combatant.



When everyone has acted, a new round begins — initiative re-rolls and everyone gets 2 fresh actions.





COMBAT ACTIONS

\--------------

Most actions cost 1 action; exceptions are noted.



&#x20; AIM                — +2 CMod on your next attack. Lost if you don't attack next.



&#x20; ATTACK             — Roll your equipped weapon. Pick a target from the dropdown (filtered by range on the tactical map). Damage applies on a hit.



&#x20; CHARGE (2 actions) — Pick a destination within 20 ft, move there, then make a melee or unarmed attack. With a ranged weapon, the charge uses unarmed instead.



&#x20; COORDINATE         — Roll Tactics. On a hit, allies within Close range (30 ft) get +2 CMod against a chosen target.



&#x20; DEFEND             — +2 defensive bonus against the next attack. Clears after one hit.



&#x20; GRAPPLE            — Opposed check on an Engaged target (5 ft).

&#x20;                      Both sides roll 2d6 + Physicality + Unarmed Combat (defender can use Athletics if higher). Win = target is Grappled (1 RP damage). Lose = you take 1 RP. Tie = nothing.

&#x20;                      A grappled character can only "Break Free" (another opposed check). The grappler can only "Release" (free action).



&#x20; MOVE               — Cells within 10 ft highlight green.

&#x20;                      Click one to move there.



&#x20; RAPID FIRE

&#x20;   (2 actions, ranged) — Two shots: first at -1 CMod, second at -3 CMod.



&#x20; READY WEAPON       — Opens a sub-modal:

&#x20;                      Switch — swap primary and secondary.

&#x20;                      Reload — uses 1 reload clip to refill ammo.

&#x20;                      Unjam / Repair — roll Tinkerer, Weaponsmith, or the relevant combat skill to fix a Damaged or Broken weapon.



&#x20; REPOSITION         — Free end-of-round positioning.



&#x20; SPRINT (2 actions) — Move 30 ft (3x normal). Requires an Athletics check.



&#x20; SUBDUE             — Non-lethal attack. Full RP damage but only 50% WP damage.



&#x20; TAKE COVER         — +2 defensive bonus against ALL attacks this round. Once per round. Enables "Fire from Cover" (2 actions: shoot while keeping cover).



&#x20; UNARMED            — Physicality + Unarmed Combat, 1d3 damage. Always available.



&#x20; STABILIZE          — Appears when an ally within 20 ft is mortally wounded. Roll Medicine to stop their death countdown. Must be Engaged (5 ft) to actually do it.





BLAST RADIUS

\------------

Grenades, RPGs, and Molotovs apply damage automatically to every token near the blast center on the tactical map:



&#x20; Engaged (5 ft)    full damage Close (30 ft)     50% damage Far (100 ft)      25% damage



The attacker is excluded from splash. Damage details show in the roll log.





MORTAL WOUNDS

\-------------

When a character's WP hits 0:

&#x20; - A death countdown starts (4 + Physicality rounds).

&#x20; - If the character has any Insight Dice, a modal asks whether to trade ALL of them to survive with 1 WP and 1 RP. Only the

&#x20;   PLAYER who owns the character can make that choice; the GM sees a read-only version.

&#x20; - If nobody stabilizes them before the countdown expires, the character dies.



Whenever a PC enters WP=0 or RP=0 — even if they're saved — they gain 1 Stress (capped at the 5-pip max).





ENDING COMBAT (GM)

\------------------

Click "End Combat" in the header. Initiative clears. Grapple states release. Mortally wounded PCs gain +1 Stress (the "survived combat" pip).





====================================================================

&#x20;9. CREATING A COMMUNITY

====================================================================



A COMMUNITY is a group of PCs and NPCs bound together as a household, settlement, band, or enclave. Communities are one of the flagship Distemper mechanics — they turn the people your party knows into a roster with morale, resources, roles, and weekly checks that shape the fiction between adventures.



The full rules are in the SRD §08 Communities. The Tapestry implements them mechanically; this section walks you through the platform UI.





GROUP vs COMMUNITY — THE 13-MEMBER THRESHOLD

\--------------------------------------------

Until a group has 13 combined members (PCs + NPCs), it's a GROUP — your adventuring party. Groups don't run weekly checks.



At 13+ members it becomes a COMMUNITY and weekly mechanics unlock:

&#x20; - Weekly Fed / Clothed / Morale checks.

&#x20; - Role minimums for the labor pool.

&#x20; - A leader who rolls Morale.

&#x20; - Failure states: 25% / 50% / 75% departures, plus dissolution after three consecutive failures.



If a Community ever drops back below 13 it reverts to a Group and weekly checks pause until it grows again.



A status chip on the community header tells you which state you're in: green "Community", amber "Group", or red "Dissolved."





CREATING A COMMUNITY (GM)

\-------------------------

On the Table, click Community ▾ in the header, then "New Community." Fill in:



&#x20; - Name (required). Short and flavorful: "The Schoolers", "Greenhouse Collective", "Mongrels East".

&#x20; - Description (optional). One or two sentences.

&#x20; - Homestead pin (optional). Pick an existing campaign pin — the community is narratively rooted there.



You're auto-enrolled as the founder and the default Leader.





ADDING AND REMOVING MEMBERS

\---------------------------

Inside an expanded community, the "Add Member" form (GM only) lets you add NPCs from your campaign roster or PCs from your player list. You pick:



&#x20; - Subject kind: NPC or PC.

&#x20; - The specific person.

&#x20; - Their role (Gatherer / Maintainer / Safety / Unassigned / Assigned — see below).

&#x20; - Their recruitment type (Member, Cohort, Conscript, Convert, Founder).



The mechanical way to bring NPCs in during play is the Recruitment Check (Chapter 10).



Remove a member with the × button on their row. Members are soft-removed (history preserved) with a reason: morale-departure, dissolution, manual, or killed.





ROLES (NPCs ONLY)

\-----------------

Every NPC member has one of five roles. PCs aren't counted toward role minimums — they're always PCs, not workers.



&#x20; GATHERER     Hunting, foraging, farming, scavenging. Rolls the weekly Fed Check.

&#x20; MAINTAINER   Repair, clothing, supplies, tools. Rolls the weekly Clothed Check.

&#x20; SAFETY       Patrols and emergency services. Modifies Morale.

&#x20;              Community leadership usually comes from here.

&#x20; UNASSIGNED   Idle. Pulls down the labor pool if anything's understaffed.

&#x20; ASSIGNED     Off doing a PC-directed mission. Doesn't count toward labor minimums. Always carries a directing

&#x20;              PC and a task description.





ROLE MINIMUMS (SRD §08)

\-----------------------

The NPC labor pool (everyone whose role is NOT Assigned) has minimum fractions:



&#x20; GATHERERS   ≥ 33%

&#x20; MAINTAINERS ≥ 20%

&#x20; SAFETY       5–10% (at least 1 if the pool is tiny)



These directly drive the weekly Morale Check via the "Enough Hands" slot:

&#x20; - All three minimums met → +1 to Morale.

&#x20; - For each role below its minimum → −1 (capped at −3).



The role bars on the community body show current vs. minimum counts. Green = OK. Red = understaffed. The "⚖ Re-balance Roles" button auto-assigns NPCs to roles based on their skills.





THE LEADER

\----------

One PC or NPC rolls the weekly Morale Check on the community's behalf. By default the founder is leader until somebody is explicitly set.



Set the leader: in the community body, the "Leader" row has a dropdown listing every member.



Stepping down: the GM (always) and the current PC leader (themselves) can abdicate via "Step Down." A successor picker opens with these options:

&#x20; - AUTO — pick the next Founder, otherwise the longest-tenured active member.

&#x20; - LEAVE LEADERLESS — clears the leader. Costs −1 on the "A Clear Voice" Morale slot every week.

&#x20; - A specific member.



If a Leader leaves entirely (removed, killed, role changed), a successor is auto-picked.





THE WEEKLY CHECK (FED → CLOTHED → MORALE)

\-----------------------------------------

Once per in-game week, the GM clicks "Run Weekly Check" on the community body. A modal fires three rolls in sequence:



&#x20; 1. FED CHECK     Gatherers roll for Rations.

&#x20; 2. CLOTHED CHECK Maintainers roll for Supplies.

&#x20; 3. MORALE CHECK  The Leader rolls.



Fed and Clothed outcomes feed directly into the Morale modifier slots — a great Fed adds +1 or +2 to Morale; a famine drags it down by −2 or worse.





THE MORALE MODIFIER SLOTS

\-------------------------

Each Morale Check has six built-in slots plus a freeform GM slot:



&#x20; 1. FED              Fed Check outcome (auto).

&#x20; 2. CLOTHED          Clothed Check outcome (auto).

&#x20; 3. MOOD AROUND THE  Last week's Morale carry-over (auto).

&#x20;    CAMPFIRE

&#x20; 4. ENOUGH HANDS     +1 if all role minimums met; -1 each group below (capped -3).

&#x20; 5. A CLEAR VOICE    0 with a leader, -1 if leaderless.

&#x20; 6. SOMEONE TO       +1 if Safety ≥10% of pool, 0 if 5-10%,

&#x20;    WATCH OVER ME    -1 below 5%.

&#x20; 7. ADDITIONAL       GM freeform — raids, miracles, weather, Distemper surges. Resets each modal open.



Hover any slot label to read the SRD rule text in a tooltip.

The GM can override any slot before rolling.





MORALE OUTCOMES

\---------------

&#x20;   Roll        Outcome          Departures   Next Mood

&#x20;   ---------------------------------------------------

&#x20;   6+6         High Insight       none          +2 14+         Wild Success       none          +1 9–13        Success            none           0 4–8         Failure            25%           −1 0–3         Dire Failure       50%           −2 1+1         Low Insight        75%           −3



Departures soft-remove NPCs (PCs are never auto-removed). The removal priority order is:



&#x20;   Unassigned → Cohort → Convert → Conscript → Founder → Apprentice (last)



Apprentices and Founders stay the longest when times get hard.



Any success resets the consecutive-failure counter to 0. Any failure increments it by 1.





DISSOLUTION ON THREE CONSECUTIVE FAILURES

\-----------------------------------------

Three failed Morale Checks in a row collapses the community. At 2 / 3 the panel shows a red warning: "one more failure dissolves the community."



If the third fail hits, the result panel offers:



&#x20; 🙏 ATTEMPT RETENTION CHECK A "fast-acting leader" rolls one immediate Morale Check using only the Mood slot. Success at any tier saves the community (consecutive\_failures drops to 2 — one week of cushion). Failure proceeds with dissolution.



&#x20; Finalize — Dissolve Community Confirm. Community goes to "dissolved", every active member is soft-removed, and "Run Weekly Check" disappears.



Dissolution is permanent. Make a new community if you want a similar story.





SKIP WEEK

\---------

"Skip Week" sits next to "Run Weekly Check." It bumps the week counter by 1 without rolling Fed / Clothed / Morale. Use it for narrative off-screen time. Mood carries over from the most recently rolled check, not the skipped one.





THE COMMUNITY DASHBOARD (GM ONLY)

\---------------------------------

Full-screen view at /stories/<id>/community (also reachable from Community ▾ → Dashboard). Per-community it shows:



&#x20; - Header summary: status, week counter, consecutive failures, member count.

&#x20; - Morale History — last 20 weeks with color-coded outcome badges and member deltas.

&#x20; - Resource History — last 40 Fed / Clothed rolls.

&#x20; - Current Role Distribution — bars vs. SRD minimum markers.

&#x20; - Recruitment Stats — success rate by approach.

&#x20; - Members by Recruitment Type — tile breakdown.



Players can't access this page.





THE AT-A-GLANCE BLOCK (PLAYER-FACING)

\-------------------------------------

At the top of every expanded community panel, a small blue block summarizes the community for the viewer:



&#x20; RECENT MORALE

&#x20;   Five colored letter chips (W / H / S / F / D / L) for the last five weekly outcomes, oldest left, newest right. Hover a chip for the week and full outcome.



&#x20; YOU

&#x20;   Your role in the community.

&#x20;   "Founder" tag if that's your recruitment type.

&#x20;   Your Apprentice (if any).

&#x20;   A list of NPCs you personally recruited.



Everyone sees this — it's how players keep track of "their" community without GM-only screens.

10\. NPCs AND RECRUITMENT

====================================================================



NPCs are everyone in the world who isn't a PC. They're managed in the right-panel NPCs tab on the Table.





CREATING AN NPC (GM)

\--------------------

Click "+ Add NPC" to open the modal:



&#x20; - Name.

&#x20; - Type:  Friendly, Goon, Foe, or Antagonist. The type auto-sets baseline RAPID values.

&#x20; - Portrait: portrait bank (red=enemy, green=ally, white= neutral), or upload an image, or pick from the library.

&#x20; - RAPID attributes (manually adjustable).

&#x20; - Skills (add from a dropdown, set the level 0-4).

&#x20; - Weapon — primary, all categories available.

&#x20; - Secondary Weapon — Foe and Antagonist types only.

&#x20; - Motivation and Complication — pick or roll.

&#x20; - Three personality words.

&#x20; - GM Notes — private, never shown to players.

&#x20; - Status — Active, Dead, or Unknown.





GENERATING AN NPC

\-----------------

Click "Generate NPC" and pick a type. The system rolls a random NPC with appropriate stats, skills, weapons, personality, and a portrait. Edit afterward if you want.





POPULATING A SCENE (GM)

\-----------------------

The "Populate" tool (under GM Tools) bulk-generates a roster of NPCs along the SRD's recommended distribution: 1 Antagonist, 2 Foes, 3 Goons, 4 Friendlies (the "1A:2F:3G:4B triangle").

Useful for filling a community or seeding a hostile location.





CLONING

\-------

Click the + button on the left side of any NPC card to clone them. The clone gets an auto-numbered name (e.g. "Kincaid's Soldier #2") and appears just below the original.





THE NPC CARD

\------------

Each card on the roster shows:

&#x20; Drag handle, Delete (×), Clone (+), name, status badge, Show / Hide button, Fight button (combat only), Map button (place / remove a token), portrait.



Click the card to open the full NPC sheet:

&#x20; - RAPID attributes (clickable for rolls during sessions).

&#x20; - WP / RP dot trackers.

&#x20; - Skills (clickable for rolls).

&#x20; - Unarmed attack button.

&#x20; - Weapon attack button.

&#x20; - Equipment attacks.

&#x20; - Stabilize button (when mortally wounded).

&#x20; - Restore button (when dead — resets to full HP).

&#x20; - Edit / Close buttons.

&#x20; - Recruit button (during an active session — see below).





REVEALING NPCs TO PLAYERS

\-------------------------

Click "Show" on an NPC card to reveal them. Players see the NPC's name, portrait, and type. Click "Hide" to remove visibility. "Show All" / "Hide All" in the header toggles all at once.





PUBLISHING TO THE WORLD LIBRARY

\-------------------------------

From the NPC edit modal, click "Publish to World Library" to submit the NPC for inclusion in the shared NPC library. Other GMs can import published NPCs into their Stories.





RECRUITMENT — BRINGING NPCs INTO A COMMUNITY

\--------------------------------------------

Recruitment is the mechanical way PCs bring NPCs into a Community during play (Chapter 9 covers Communities). Open the Recruit modal three ways:



&#x20; - NPC card → "Recruit" button (active session only).

&#x20; - Community ▾ → Recruit.

&#x20; - Checks ▾ → Recruit.



The modal has three steps: pick an APPROACH and a SKILL → roll

→ result.



The roll is:



&#x20;   2d6 + AMod (Influence) + SMod (your level in the chosen skill) + CMod (First Impression + any other situational mods, including Insight Dice bonuses)





THE THREE APPROACHES

\--------------------

&#x20; COHORT     Shared interest or goal. The NPC joins until the next Morale Check, then re-evaluates.



&#x20; CONSCRIPT  Coerced service. Requires a CREDIBLE THREAT — weapons drawn, leverage held, escape cut off. The modal will block the roll until you confirm the threat is real.



&#x20; CONVERT    Shared belief, ideology, or vision. The NPC joins as a probationary Convert; commits fully after surviving the next Morale Check.





APPRENTICES (FOURTH OPTION, SPECIAL)

\------------------------------------

A fourth recruitment type — APPRENTICE — is unlocked ONLY when your Recruitment Check rolls a Moment of High Insight (double-6).

Wild Success (plain 14+) does not count.



One Apprentice per PC, ever. They're a special bond:

&#x20; - They come with a name, age, randomly chosen Motivations and Complications, and 3 words.

&#x20; - They come with a setting-appropriate Profession and 1 CDP in each vocational skill.

&#x20; - Players may spend 3 CDP on RAPID attributes.

&#x20; - They may spend 5 CDP on skills.

&#x20; - The player Fills In The Gaps about their background.

&#x20; - Over one in-game month players can train the Apprentice in any one skill up to (your skill level − 1).

&#x20; - Future CDP your character earns can be spent on the Apprentice instead.



The Apprentice bond survives Morale-driven departures — they stay with you unless you die, dismiss them, or they're killed.



In the community roster, Apprentice rows show a bond line:

&#x20;   JESS BROOKER ⇐ ADA MAREN     \[Unassigned ▾]  × Task: Training with Ada in the garage   ✎ edit





PRESSGANGING — THE CREDIBLE-THREAT GATE

\---------------------------------------

The SRD says: "The PCs must present a credible threat for Conscription to work."



When you pick Conscript on the Recruit modal, a red banner appears under the approach tiles. When you click Roll, a blocking confirm dialog asks you to acknowledge: the PC has established a credible threat. Cancel = no roll, no resources burned. Cohort and Convert never show this gate.

The Campfire is the cross-Story community hub. Click "The Campfire" in the sidebar to land on it.



It's a single page with tabs:



&#x20; LOOKING FOR GROUP   (default tab)

&#x20; FORUMS

&#x20; WAR STORIES

&#x20; TIMESTAMPS



All four pages are public to logged-in users — no Story or campaign membership required.





LOOKING FOR GROUP — /campfire/lfg

\---------------------------------

A bulletin board for finding GMs / players.



Post a listing as either:

&#x20; - GM seeking players, or

&#x20; - Player seeking game.



Each post has a title, body, optional setting tag (campaign flavor), and an optional schedule note.



Other people click "I'm Interested" to express interest. As the post author, you see a roster of who's interested and can DM them directly (or, if you're a GM, send them a Story invite).

A Share button lets you push the post to Reddit, Twitter, Facebook, or copy a direct link.



You can edit and delete your own posts. You can't see other people's interest rosters.

Threaded discussions organized by category (Lore, Rules, Session Recaps, General).



&#x20; - Click "+ New Thread" to start one. Title + body.

&#x20; - Click any thread to read it; reply at the bottom.

&#x20; - Edit and delete your own posts.

&#x20; - Threads sort by most-recent reply.



Pinned threads (set by Thrivers) float to the top. Locked threads accept no new replies.



There's also a /campfire/forums2 URL — that's a Reddit-style preview UI on the same data, with up/down voting. The voting is session-only (resets on refresh) and the page is labeled "Preview · Style B". Treat it as experimental.





WAR STORIES — /campfire/war-stories

\-----------------------------------

A cross-Story feed for session highlights — the time the party blew up the wrong building, the time someone rolled double-6 on the Recruitment Check, etc.



&#x20; - Title + body.

&#x20; - Optional campaign tag (so readers know which Story it came from).

&#x20; - Attach images and PDFs.

&#x20; - Edit and delete your own posts.



If War Stories isn't available on your install, the page shows a banner — your Thriver needs to run the war\_stories migration.





TIMESTAMPS — /campfire/timestamp

\--------------------------------

A utility tool. Pick a date, time, and timezone — the page returns a Discord-compatible timestamp token (looks like `<t:1234567890:F>`). Paste it in Discord and it renders as the local time for every viewer.



Six format options plus raw Unix seconds. Useful for scheduling sessions across timezones.

12\. RUMORS

====================================================================



A RUMOR is a published, reusable bundle of adventure content — NPCs, pins, tactical scenes, and handouts — that one GM has authored and made available for other GMs to drop into their own Stories. Think of it like an adventure pack, except authored entirely inside The Tapestry instead of through PDFs and prep notes.



Rumors are how content travels between Stories. They're the distribution layer that lets one GM build "The Mongrels' first month" or "the District Zero docks heist" once, then ship it.



Click "Rumors" in the sidebar to browse them. The same content is referred to as "modules" in some technical UI (the URL is /modules; the publish button says "📦 Publish Module"). They mean the same thing.



\[screenshot: Rumors list with module cards]





WHAT'S IN A RUMOR

\-----------------

Each Rumor packages:

&#x20; - NPCs (stats, portraits, skills, equipment).

&#x20; - Map pins (campaign-map locations with notes).

&#x20; - Tactical scenes (battle maps with grid + object tokens).

&#x20; - Handouts (campaign notes with attachments).



It does NOT package:

&#x20; - Player characters (PCs stay with players).

&#x20; - Per-session state (HP, combat round, etc.).

&#x20; - Community rosters (yet).

&#x20; - Session logs or roll history.



Each publish creates an immutable VERSION (v1.0.0, v1.1.0, ...).

Subscribers clone a version into their own Story; the clone is theirs to edit. When you publish a new version later, subscribed Stories see an "Update available" prompt.





PUBLISHING A RUMOR (GM)

\-----------------------

1\. Build out a Story like normal — add NPCs, drop pins, build tactical scenes, write handouts. Whatever exists in the Story when you click Publish is what travels.

&#x20;  (Tip: name your authoring Story something like "Mongrels — Source" so you know it's the authoring copy, not a play copy.)



2\. Go to your Story's page (/stories/<id>). Click the purple "📦 Publish Module" button.



3\. Fill in the modal:

&#x20;  - Module Name      — prefilled from your Story name; you can rename here. This is what subscribers see.

&#x20;  - Tagline          — optional one-liner.

&#x20;  - Description      — optional paragraph.

&#x20;  - Visibility — three tiers:

&#x20;      🔒 PRIVATE   Only you can pick it. Skips moderation.

&#x20;                   Use for drafts.

&#x20;      🔗 UNLISTED  Anyone with the link can pick it. Skips moderation. Use for sharing with a co-GM.

&#x20;      🌐 LISTED    Public marketplace listing. A Thriver must approve it. Goes "pending" until then.

&#x20;  - Content — four checkboxes (Pins, NPCs, Tactical Scenes, Handouts) with live counts. Uncheck anything you don't want to ship — usually spoiler pins or WIP NPCs.



4\. Hit "📦 Publish v1.0.0." A few seconds later the button re-labels to "📦 Module v1.0.0" — your Rumor is live.





USING A RUMOR (GM)

\------------------

When creating a new Story (Chapter 5), the form has a section labeled "OR START FROM A MODULE" below the Setting picker. It lists every Rumor visible to you:



&#x20; - Your own Rumors at any visibility.

&#x20; - Anyone's LISTED + approved Rumors.

&#x20; - UNLISTED Rumors you've been given a link to.



Click a Rumor to select it (it highlights purple). Picking a Rumor clears any Setting you had — they're mutually exclusive.



Fill in the rest of the form and create the Story. The Tapestry clones the Rumor's snapshot — every NPC, pin, scene, and handout — into your new Story with fresh IDs. The clone is yours: edit it freely.





PUBLISHING A NEW VERSION

\------------------------

After v1.0, the Publish button on your Story page reads "📦 Module v1.0.0." Click it to ship a new version.



The wizard flips to "Publish New Version." You pick a SEMVER bump:

&#x20; PATCH → v1.0.1   Silent fixes, typos, stat tweaks.

&#x20; MINOR → v1.1.0   New content, backwards-compatible (default).

&#x20; MAJOR → v2.0.0   Breaking — renamed scenes, removed NPCs.



You write a CHANGELOG (free-form release notes — "added 3 NPCs to the south wing, rewrote the Costco handout"). Subscribers see this verbatim.



Hit Publish. Every Story subscribed to your Rumor gets a bell notification.





RECEIVING AN UPDATE (SUBSCRIBER GM)

\-----------------------------------

Two ways you'll know a Rumor has updated:



&#x20; - The notification bell fires:

&#x20;       📦 MODULE UPDATED A new version of "<rumor name>" (v1.1.0) is available: <changelog> Click the notification to jump to the version history.



&#x20; - On your Story page, a purple "📦 v1.1.0 ↑" button appears in the GM action row. Click it to see what changed.



THE VERSION HISTORY PAGE

&#x20; /stories/<id>/modules/<module-id>/versions



Lists every published version, newest first. Each version card shows:

&#x20; - Version number, published timestamp.

&#x20; - Author's changelog (if any).

&#x20; - One-line diff vs. the prior version ("+2 NPCs, -1 pin, 3 handouts updated").

&#x20; - Subscriber count chip.



Your currently-cloned version gets a purple "Your clone" chip.

For now this page is read-only awareness — one-click "apply this update to my Story" is on the roadmap.





RUMORS vs. PINNED RUMORS — DON'T CONFUSE THEM

\---------------------------------------------

"Rumor" means two related things on The Tapestry. They're not the same:



&#x20; - The sidebar "Rumors" link → published adventure content (this chapter).

&#x20; - The "Rumor" pin category on the world map → a player- submitted location pending Thriver moderation (Chapters 2 and 3).



The thematic overlap is intentional (Distemper survivors share rumors of places, dangers, and opportunities — and these rumors are the seeds of adventures), but the two systems are mechanically separate. The pin is a single point on a map; a published Rumor is a full adventure pack.
