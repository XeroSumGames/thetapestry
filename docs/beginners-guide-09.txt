====================================================================
 9. CREATING A COMMUNITY
====================================================================

A COMMUNITY is a group of PCs and NPCs bound together as a household, settlement, band, or enclave. Communities are one of the flagship Distemper mechanics — they turn the people your party knows into a roster with morale, resources, roles, and weekly checks that shape the fiction between adventures.

The full rules live in the Xero Sum Engine SRD §08 Communities. This chapter walks you through the platform UI.


GROUP vs COMMUNITY — THE 13-MEMBER THRESHOLD
--------------------------------------------
Until a group has 13 combined members (PCs + NPCs), it's a GROUP — your adventuring party. Groups don't run weekly checks.

At 13+ members it becomes a COMMUNITY and weekly mechanics unlock:
  - Weekly Fed / Clothed / Morale checks.
  - Role minimums for the labor pool.
  - A leader who rolls Morale.
  - Failure states: 25% / 50% / 75% departures, plus dissolution after three consecutive failures.

If a Community drops back below 13 it reverts to a Group and weekly checks pause until it grows again.

A status chip on the community header tells you which state you're in: green Community, amber Group, or red Dissolved.


CREATING A COMMUNITY (GM)
-------------------------
On the Table, click Community ▾ in the header, then "New Community." Fill in:

  - Name (required). Short and flavorful: "The Schoolers", "Greenhouse Collective", "Mongrels East".
  - Description (optional). One or two sentences.
  - Homestead pin (optional). Pick an existing campaign pin to root the community there.

You're auto-enrolled as the founder and default Leader.


ADDING AND REMOVING MEMBERS
---------------------------
The "Add Member" form (GM only) lets you add NPCs from your campaign roster or PCs from your player list. You pick:

  - Subject kind: NPC or PC.
  - The specific person.
  - Their role: Gatherer / Maintainer / Safety / Unassigned / Assigned (see below).
  - Their recruitment type: Member, Cohort, Conscript, Convert, or Founder.

The mechanical way to bring NPCs in during play is the Recruitment Check (Chapter 10).

Remove a member with the × button. Members are soft-removed (history preserved) with a reason: morale-departure, dissolution, manual, or killed.


ROLES (NPCs ONLY)
-----------------
Every NPC member has one of five roles. PCs aren't counted toward role minimums — they're always PCs, not workers.

  GATHERER     Hunting, foraging, farming, scavenging. Rolls the weekly Fed Check.
  MAINTAINER   Repair, clothing, supplies, tools. Rolls the weekly Clothed Check.
  SAFETY       Patrols and emergency services. Modifies Morale. Community leadership usually comes from here.
  UNASSIGNED   Idle. Pulls down the labor pool if anything's understaffed.
  ASSIGNED     Off doing a PC-directed mission. Doesn't count toward labor minimums. Always carries a directing PC and a task description.


ROLE MINIMUMS (SRD §08)
-----------------------
The NPC labor pool (everyone whose role is NOT Assigned) has minimum fractions:

  GATHERERS   ≥ 33%
  MAINTAINERS ≥ 20%
  SAFETY       5–10% (at least 1 if the pool is tiny)

These directly drive the weekly Morale Check via the "Enough Hands" slot:
  - All three minimums met → +1 to Morale.
  - For each role below its minimum → −1 (capped at −3).

Role bars on the community body show current vs. minimum counts. Green = OK. Red = understaffed. The "⚖ Re-balance Roles" button auto-assigns NPCs to roles based on their skills.


THE LEADER
----------
One PC or NPC rolls the weekly Morale Check on the community's behalf. By default the founder is leader until somebody is explicitly set.

Set the leader: in the community body, the "Leader" row has a dropdown listing every member.

Stepping down: the GM (always) and the current PC leader (themselves) can abdicate via "Step Down." A successor picker opens with these options:
  - AUTO — pick the next Founder, otherwise the longest-tenured active member.
  - LEAVE LEADERLESS — clears the leader. Costs −1 on the "A Clear Voice" Morale slot every week.
  - A specific member.

If a Leader leaves entirely (removed, killed, role changed), a successor is auto-picked.


THE WEEKLY CHECK
----------------
Once per in-game week, the GM clicks "Run Weekly Check." A modal fires three rolls in sequence:

  1. FED CHECK     Gatherers roll for Rations.
  2. CLOTHED CHECK Maintainers roll for Supplies.
  3. MORALE CHECK  The Leader rolls.

Fed and Clothed outcomes feed directly into the Morale modifier slots — a great Fed adds +1 or +2 to Morale; a famine drags it down by −2 or worse.


THE MORALE MODIFIER SLOTS
-------------------------
Each Morale Check has six built-in slots plus a freeform GM slot:

  1. FED                       Fed Check outcome (auto).
  2. CLOTHED                   Clothed Check outcome (auto).
  3. MOOD AROUND THE CAMPFIRE  Last week's Morale carry-over (auto).
  4. ENOUGH HANDS              +1 if all role minimums met; -1 each group below (capped -3).
  5. A CLEAR VOICE             0 with a leader, -1 if leaderless.
  6. SOMEONE TO WATCH OVER ME  +1 if Safety ≥10% of pool, 0 if 5-10%, -1 below 5%.
  7. ADDITIONAL                GM freeform — raids, miracles, weather, Distemper surges. Resets each modal open.

Hover any slot label to read the SRD rule text in a tooltip. The GM can override any slot before rolling.


MORALE OUTCOMES
---------------
    Roll        Outcome           Departures   Next Mood
    -----------------------------------------------------
    6+6         High Insight        none          +2
    14+         Wild Success        none          +1
    9–13        Success             none           0
    4–8         Failure             25%           −1
    0–3         Dire Failure        50%           −2
    1+1         Low Insight         75%           −3

Departures soft-remove NPCs (PCs are never auto-removed). Removal priority order:

    Unassigned → Cohort → Convert → Conscript → Founder → Apprentice (last)

Apprentices and Founders stay the longest when times get hard.

Any success resets the consecutive-failure counter to 0. Any failure increments it by 1.


DISSOLUTION ON THREE CONSECUTIVE FAILURES
-----------------------------------------
Three failed Morale Checks in a row collapses the community. At 2 of 3, the panel shows a red warning: "one more failure dissolves the community."

If the third fail hits, the result panel offers two paths:

  🙏 ATTEMPT RETENTION CHECK
    A "fast-acting leader" rolls one immediate Morale Check using only the Mood slot. Success at any tier saves the community; consecutive_failures drops to 2 — one week of cushion. Failure proceeds with dissolution.

  Finalize — Dissolve Community
    Confirm. Community goes to Dissolved, every active member is soft-removed, and "Run Weekly Check" disappears.

Dissolution is permanent. Make a new community if you want a similar story.


SKIP WEEK
---------
"Skip Week" sits next to "Run Weekly Check." It bumps the week counter by 1 without rolling Fed / Clothed / Morale. Use it for narrative off-screen time. Mood carries over from the most recently rolled check, not the skipped one.


THE COMMUNITY DASHBOARD (GM ONLY)
---------------------------------
A full-screen view at /stories/<id>/community, also reachable from Community ▾ → Dashboard. Per-community it shows:

  - Header summary: status, week counter, consecutive failures, member count.
  - Morale History — last 20 weeks with color-coded outcome badges and member deltas.
  - Resource History — last 40 Fed / Clothed rolls.
  - Current Role Distribution — bars vs. SRD minimum markers.
  - Recruitment Stats — success rate by approach.
  - Members by Recruitment Type — tile breakdown.

Players can't access this page.


THE AT-A-GLANCE BLOCK (PLAYER-FACING)
-------------------------------------
At the top of every expanded community panel, a small blue block summarizes the community for the viewer:

  RECENT MORALE
    Five colored letter chips for the last five weekly outcomes, oldest left, newest right. Hover a chip for the week and full outcome.

  YOU
    Your role in the community.
    "Founder" tag if that's your recruitment type.
    Your Apprentice (if any).
    A list of NPCs you personally recruited.

Everyone sees this block — it's how players keep track of "their" community without GM-only screens.
