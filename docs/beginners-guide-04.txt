====================================================================
 4. CREATING A CHARACTER
====================================================================

In Distemper a character is called a SURVIVOR. The Tapestry has four ways to make one, all listed under "Survivors" in the sidebar:

  BACKSTORY GENERATION   life-pathing method, most authored.
  PARADIGMS              pick a 1-click template, then tune.
  QUICK CHARACTER        fast, truncated build.
  RANDOM CHARACTER       1 click. Done.

All four produce the same kind of character. They differ only in how much you author up front.


WHAT'S IN A CHARACTER
---------------------
A few terms you'll see throughout:

  RAPID — five core attributes: Reason, Acumen, Physicality, Influence, Dexterity. Range −2 to +5; newcomers usually end up with values from 0 to +3. Full descriptions live in the Xero Sum Engine SRD.

  SKILLS — trained abilities like Melee Combat, Medicine, Tactics, etc. Each skill has a level 0-4. The SRD lists all skills.

  CDP (Character Development Points) — your build budget. Spend CDP to raise RAPID attributes and skills.

  MOTIVATION and COMPLICATION — narrative anchors. Why your character keeps going, and what's broken about them. Pick from a list or roll randomly.

  WP (Wound Points) and RP (Resilience Points) — the two health tracks. WP is hard physical damage; RP is fatigue and bruises. WP at 0 = mortally wounded. RP at 0 = unconscious.

  STRESS — a 5-pip track. Hitting pip 5 forces a Stress check. Stress is added when bad things happen, including hitting WP=0 or RP=0.

  INSIGHT DICE — earned on extreme dice rolls (double 1s or double 6s). Spend one to roll 3d6 (keep best 2) instead of 2d6, OR add +3 to your roll's situational modifier.

  WEAPONS — every character has a Primary and Secondary slot; one melee, one ranged is the usual pairing. Each weapon has damage, range, ammo, and a condition track.


BACKSTORY GENERATION
--------------------
A life-pathing wizard. Walk your character through their life — childhood, education, hobbies, career, what they learned after the Dog Flu — and spend Character Development Points at each phase. The result is a deeply authored Survivor with rooted history.

You'll close out by picking a Motivation and Complication, watching your secondary stats auto-calculate, choosing primary and secondary weapons plus equipment, and reviewing.

PARADIGMS
---------
A Paradigm is an archetype — a pre-built RAPID and skill loadout that the SRD ships with. There are 12, including Soldier, Doctor, and Mechanic. Pick one and the system creates a character seeded with that template, plus a rolled Motivation and Complication.

You land on the Final Review step where you can rebalance attributes and skills before saving. Standard creation caps apply: no attribute or skill above +3.

[screenshot: Paradigms picker grid]


QUICK CHARACTER
---------------
A fast, truncated build. You get 5 Attribute CDP and 15 Skill CDP and spend everything at once, with an optional profession preset to start you off. Pick a Motivation and Complication, kit out, save.


RANDOM CHARACTER
----------------
One click. The system rolls a complete character — name, attributes, skills, weapons, gear, motivation, complication, and a portrait — saves it automatically, and opens the edit page in case you want to tweak anything.


MANAGING YOUR CHARACTERS
------------------------
"My Survivors" in the sidebar shows all your characters. Each card displays:

  - Name, profession, three descriptive words.
  - Motivation and Complication.
  - Portrait (click to enlarge).
  - RAPID attributes.
  - Skills grid.
  - Weapons (primary and secondary, with traits and ammo).
  - Live trackers: WP, RP, Stress, Insight Dice, CDP, Morality.

Edit any field. Print a sheet. Duplicate to copy a character. Delete is permanent (asks first).


HEALTH TRACKERS
---------------
Click WP and RP circles to take or heal damage. Click Stress pips the same way. Insight Dice are earned automatically by your rolls; you spend them from the roll modal at the table.

Reaching 0 WP starts a death countdown of 4 + Physicality rounds. A nearby ally has to roll Medicine to stabilize you (Chapter 8). If you have any Insight Dice, the system asks whether to spend them ALL to survive with 1 WP and 1 RP. That's a one-way ticket — only the player can make that choice.

Reaching 0 RP makes you unconscious. Any time you reach 0 in either track, you also gain 1 Stress.


WEAPONS
-------
Each weapon has:
  - Skill used (Melee Combat, Ranged Combat, Athletics for explosives, Demolitions for placed charges).
  - Damage formula.
  - RP percentage (how much of the damage is RP vs. WP).
  - Range band: Engaged 5 ft, Close 30 ft, Mid 100 ft, Far 200 ft+.
  - Condition: Pristine +1, Used 0, Worn −1, Damaged −2, Broken (unusable).
  - Ammo and reload clips (ranged weapons only).
  - Weapon traits like Burst, Two-Handed, Loud.
