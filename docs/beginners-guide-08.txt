====================================================================
 8. COMBAT
====================================================================

Combat is a structured mode you enter from the Table during an active session. The GM starts and ends it.


STARTING COMBAT (GM)
--------------------
1. Click "Start Combat" in the Table header.
2. Pick which NPCs are in this fight.
3. Optionally choose a "Drop" character — they act first, alone, before the rest of the round.
4. Every combatant rolls initiative: 2d6 + Acumen + Dexterity.
5. The initiative bar appears below the header, sorted highest to lowest.
6. The tactical map auto-shares to players.

[screenshot: initiative bar with portraits + numbers]


THE INITIATIVE BAR
------------------
Shows everyone in turn order — portrait, name, initiative roll — with a highlight on whoever's turn it is. The round counter sits next to the bar.

The GM can click X on any combatant to remove them or skip their turn.


TURNS AND ROUNDS
----------------
Every combatant gets 2 ACTIONS per turn. The active combatant's action bar appears below the initiative order. When both actions are spent (or the GM clicks Next Turn), play moves to the next combatant.

When everyone has acted, a new round begins — initiative re-rolls and everyone gets 2 fresh actions.


COMBAT ACTIONS
--------------
Most actions cost 1 action; exceptions are noted.

  AIM                — +2 CMod on your next attack. Lost if you don't attack next.

  ATTACK             — Roll your equipped weapon. Pick a target from the dropdown (filtered by range on the tactical map). Damage applies on a hit.

  CHARGE (2 actions) — Pick a destination within 20 ft, move there, then make a melee or unarmed attack. Charging with a ranged weapon downgrades to unarmed.

  COORDINATE         — Roll Tactics. On a hit, allies within Close range (30 ft) get +2 CMod against a chosen target.

  DEFEND             — +2 defensive bonus against the next attack. Clears after one hit.

  GRAPPLE            — Opposed check on an Engaged target (5 ft). Both sides roll 2d6 + Physicality + Unarmed Combat (defender can use Athletics if higher). Win = target is Grappled and takes 1 RP. Lose = you take 1 RP. Tie = nothing.
                       A grappled character can only "Break Free" (another opposed check). The grappler can only "Release" (free action).

  MOVE               — Cells within 10 ft highlight green. Click one to move there.

  RAPID FIRE
    (2 actions, ranged)  Two shots: first at -1 CMod, second at -3 CMod.

  READY WEAPON       — Opens a sub-modal:
                       Switch — swap primary and secondary.
                       Reload — uses 1 reload clip to refill ammo.
                       Unjam / Repair — roll Tinkerer, Weaponsmith, or the relevant combat skill to fix a Damaged or Broken weapon.

  REPOSITION         — Free end-of-round positioning.

  SPRINT (2 actions) — Move 30 ft (3x normal). Requires an Athletics check.

  SUBDUE             — Non-lethal attack. Full RP damage, only 50% WP damage.

  TAKE COVER         — +2 defensive bonus against ALL attacks this round. Once per round. Enables "Fire from Cover" (2 actions: shoot while keeping cover).

  UNARMED            — Physicality + Unarmed Combat, 1d3 damage. Always available.

  STABILIZE          — Appears when an ally within 20 ft is mortally wounded. Roll Medicine to stop their death countdown. Must be Engaged (5 ft) to actually do it.


BLAST RADIUS
------------
Grenades, RPGs, and Molotovs apply damage automatically to every token near the blast center on the tactical map:

  Engaged (5 ft)    full damage
  Close (30 ft)     50% damage
  Far (100 ft)      25% damage

The attacker is excluded from splash. Damage details show in the roll log.


MORTAL WOUNDS
-------------
When a character's WP hits 0:
  - A death countdown starts (4 + Physicality rounds).
  - If the character has any Insight Dice, a modal asks whether to trade ALL of them to survive with 1 WP and 1 RP. Only the player who owns the character can make that choice; the GM sees a read-only version.
  - If nobody stabilizes them before the countdown expires, the character dies.

Whenever a PC enters WP=0 or RP=0 — even if they're saved — they gain 1 Stress (capped at 5).


ENDING COMBAT (GM)
------------------
Click "End Combat" in the header. Initiative clears. Grapple states release. Mortally wounded PCs gain +1 Stress (the "survived combat" pip).
