====================================================================
 1. OPENING THE TACTICAL MAP
====================================================================

From the Table, click "Tactical Map" in the header to swap from the Campaign Map.

If your Story has no scenes yet, the Create Scene modal opens automatically. If scenes exist, the most recent one activates.


====================================================================
 2. CREATING A SCENE
====================================================================

Click "Map Setup" in the Table header to open the scene controls in a separate window. New scenes start there too — there's a "+ New Scene" button at the top.

Fill in:

  - Name (required).
  - Grid dimensions — 20 columns × 15 rows by default.
  - Cell size in feet — 3 ft per cell by default. This drives every range calculation downstream.

A new scene auto-activates and broadcasts to anyone watching the Table.


====================================================================
 3. THE GRID
====================================================================

The grid is what positions everything: tokens, range circles, fog cells, line of sight.

Cells use Chebyshev distance — the greater of the X and Y deltas. A diagonal step counts as 1, not 1.4. This matches the SRD's tabletop ranges.

Columns are labeled A, B, C... and roll into AA past Z. Rows are numbered 1, 2, 3...

SCENE CONTROLS (the "Map Setup" popout)
  Scene dropdown   — switch between scenes or create a new one.
  Background image — upload a JPG or PNG. Scale it with the four corner handles.
  Cols / Rows      — adjust grid dimensions.
  Cell (ft)        — feet per cell.
  Cell (px)        — pixel size on screen.
  Grid ON/OFF      — toggle the overlay.
  Grid color       — White, Black, or Grey.
  Grid opacity     — slider.
  Lock / Unlock    — locks the background image so it can't be moved by accident.
  Fit to Map       — zooms the view to fit the full scene.
  Edit Fog 🌫️     — enters fog paint mode. See Chapter 10.

The popout sits in its own window so you can keep it docked beside the main view while you work.


====================================================================
 4. TOKENS — PCs, NPCs, AND OBJECTS
====================================================================

Three kinds of tokens live on the tactical map:

  PC TOKENS
    Round, blue border. The owning player drags their own; the GM can drag any. Each shows a portrait or initials, name, WP bar, and — in combat — an initiative badge.

  NPC TOKENS
    Round, red border. GM-only drag. Hidden from players by default until combat starts.

  OBJECT TOKENS
    Square, orange border. Multi-cell footprint for things like crates and vehicles. Three special object types — walls, doors, and windows — shape the battlefield (see next chapter).

Click a token to select it and bring up range circles and an info panel. Double-click to open its character sheet (PC) or NPC card. Right-click — or long-press on touch — for contextual actions during combat.


====================================================================
 5. WALLS, DOORS, AND WINDOWS
====================================================================

Three special object tokens shape both movement AND fog of war's line of sight.

  WALLS    Stone-brick fill, brown border. Block movement and vision. Use them for building walls, cliff faces, barricades.

  DOORS    Red border when closed, green dashed border when open. Block movement when closed. Block vision when closed; pass vision when open. Players click a door to toggle it.

  WINDOWS  Glass fill with a cross mullion. Block movement (no walking through glass) but always pass vision. A PC behind a window sees through it, and fog clears beyond.

Together these let you build sealed rooms, doorways your players have to commit to opening, and sight lines through glass — without writing line-of-sight rules into your prep notes.

A token that tries to move into a closed door's cell gets a popup: "That door is closed. Open it first."


====================================================================
 6. RANGE AND MOVEMENT
====================================================================

Selecting a token brings up three concentric range circles around it:

  ENGAGED (5 ft)    Green. The melee radius. Required for grapples, melee attacks, and stabilizing a fallen ally.
  MOVE (9 ft)       Blue. How far you can move on one Move action.
  WEAPON RANGE      Red. The reach of your selected weapon — Engaged, Close (30 ft), Mid (100 ft), or Far (200+ ft).

The GM sees these circles around every selected token. Players see them only around their own PC, so NPC weapon ranges aren't given away.

Movement during combat costs 1 action and is bounded by your Move circle. Outside combat it's free — just drag the token wherever.


====================================================================
 7. PINGING
====================================================================

Hold ALT and left-click any cell to drop a ping — a pulsing flash visible to everyone watching the scene.

  GM ping      Orange.
  Player ping  Green.

Pings are the fastest way to point at a thing without typing in chat. They fade in about a second.


====================================================================
 8. SHARING THE SCENE
====================================================================

Players see the tactical map only when the GM has shared it.

Combat starts a share automatically. Outside combat, click "Share Map" in the Table header to push the current scene to everyone. Switching to a different scene auto-broadcasts the switch — players follow you through.


====================================================================
 9. MULTIPLE SCENES
====================================================================

A Story can hold as many scenes as you want — one per encounter, one per floor of a building, one per rumor location. Switch between them with the Scene dropdown in the popout.

Need a token to follow your party between scenes? Select the token, click "→ Scene," and pick the destination. The token resets to position (1,1) on arrival so it can't land off-map when grid dimensions differ.


====================================================================
 10. FOG OF WAR — PAINTING AND ERASING
====================================================================

FOG OF WAR is a layer of darkness you, the GM, paint over the tactical map. Players see fogged cells as solid black — no terrain, no tokens, no labels behind them.

When a PC walks close enough, the fog clears around them. When they walk away, it stays cleared — fog doesn't grow back behind a moving party. You repaint manually whenever you want.

ENTERING EDIT MODE
  Click "🌫️ Edit Fog" in the scene controls. The toolbar expands and the canvas switches to paint mode. While you author, fog renders at a lighter dimming so you can see the terrain beneath. Players still see it at full darkness.

THREE TOOLS
  PAINT      Click and drag to fog cells one at a time.
  RECTANGLE  Click and drag to fog every cell inside a rectangle in one stroke.
  ERASE      Click and drag to clear fog off cells.

BULK ACTIONS
  Fog All    Fogs the entire scene in one click.
  Clear All  Clears all fog in one click.

Click "Done" to exit edit mode.

Fog stays painted across reloads, reconnects, and session boundaries. It clears only when you erase it.


====================================================================
 11. PLAYER VISION — HOW FOG REVEALS
====================================================================

Each PC token clears 6 cells of fog around itself in every direction — a circle of light that travels with the player.

That circle respects the world:

  WALLS                       Block vision. Fog stays behind them.
  CLOSED DOORS                Block vision. Fog stays behind them.
  OPEN DOORS                  Pass vision.
  WINDOWS                     Pass vision — PCs see through glass.
  OTHER PCs / NPCs / objects  Don't block vision.

A player watching the map sees:
  - Everything outside the fog.
  - Everything inside their own PCs' 6-cell circles, line-of-sight permitting.

Tokens inside fog are completely hidden — no name, no portrait, no silhouette. As a PC moves close, the fog clears, and the tokens pop in.


====================================================================
 12. COMBAT
====================================================================

Combat interacts with the tactical map and fog in a few specific ways:

  - Starting combat auto-shares the current scene with all players.
  - Hidden NPCs flip to visible when combat starts, so their initiative badges aren't anonymous.
  - Movement during combat costs 1 action and is bounded by the Move circle.
  - Fog of War does NOT auto-clear when combat ends. If you've painted fog around the encounter, your players will still walk through it on the way out — erase it manually if you want a clean exit.

End combat from the Table header. Initiative clears, grapple states release, and players resume free token dragging.
