1. WHAT IS A COMMUNITY?
-----------------------
A Community is a group of Player Characters (PCs) and NPCs bound
together as a household, settlement, band, or enclave. Communities
are one of the flagship mechanics of the XSE / Distemper system:
they turn your PC party's allies from loose contacts into a roster
with morale, resources, roles, and weekly rolls that shape the
fiction between adventures.

Every Community has:
  - A name and description (GM-authored).
  - A Homestead — optional pin on the campaign map.
  - A Leader — a PC or NPC the community recognizes.
  - A roster of members — PCs + NPCs, each with a role and a
    recruitment type (how they came to join).
  - A week counter and a consecutive-failure counter (for Morale).


2. GROUP vs COMMUNITY — THE 13-MEMBER THRESHOLD
-----------------------------------------------
A party of PCs is a "Group." Groups do not run weekly checks and
don't need roles — they're just your adventuring party.

Once the combined PC + NPC count reaches thirteen (13) members,
the Group becomes a "Community" and the weekly mechanics unlock:

  - Weekly Morale / Fed / Clothed checks.
  - Role minimums kick in (Gatherers 33%, Maintainers 20%,
    Safety 5–10%).
  - A leader is required (being leaderless costs morale).
  - Failure states (25% / 50% / 75% departures, dissolution after
    three consecutive failures).

If a Community ever drops back below 13 it reverts to a Group
and suspends weekly checks until it grows again.

The status chip on the community header tells you which state
you're in — "Group" (amber) or "Community" (green). Dissolved
communities show a red "Dissolved" chip.


3. CREATING A COMMUNITY
-----------------------
On the table page, click the  Community ▾  menu in the header.
Choose either:

  * Status       — opens the Community panel and auto-expands
                   your first community (if any exist).
  * New Community — opens the same panel with a "+ New Community"
                   form already visible.

Fill in:
  - Name (required). Short and flavorful: "The Schoolers",
    "Greenhouse Collective", "Mongrels East".
  - Description (optional). One or two sentences on who these
    people are and why they're together.
  - Homestead pin (optional). Select an existing campaign pin
    on the map; the community is narratively rooted there.
    You can add this later.

Click Create. You're auto-enrolled as a founding member with
recruitment type "Founder". You'll also be the default Leader
until one is explicitly set.


4. ADDING AND REMOVING MEMBERS
------------------------------
Inside an expanded community body, the "Add Member" form lets
the GM add NPCs from the campaign's NPC roster or PCs from the
campaign's player characters. You pick:

  - Subject kind: NPC or PC.
  - The specific person.
  - Their initial role (see section 5).
  - Their recruitment type (usually "Member" unless you mean
    something narrative — Cohort / Conscript / Convert / Founder).

Recruitment Checks (section 9) are the mechanical way PCs bring
NPCs in during play; manual "Add Member" is for setup and quick
GM adjustments.

Removing a member uses the × button on their row. Members are
soft-removed (their history stays in the DB) with a reason:

  - "morale_25" / "morale_50" / "morale_75" — weekly departures.
  - "dissolved" — community collapse.
  - "manual" — GM hand-removal.
  - "killed" — set by other systems (combat, etc.).

When you remove the Leader, the next successor is auto-picked
(see section 8).


5. ROLES
--------
Every member has one of five roles:

  GATHERER     Hunt / forage / farm / fish / scavenge for Rations.
               Rolls the weekly Fed Check.

  MAINTAINER   Repair, clothing, supplies, tools, vehicles.
               Rolls the weekly Clothed Check.

  SAFETY       Policing, patrol, fire, emergency services.
               Community leadership is drawn from here.
               No weekly check; modifies Morale.

  UNASSIGNED   Idle / no assigned duty. Pulls down "Enough Hands"
               if the community is understaffed elsewhere.

  ASSIGNED     Off doing a PC-directed mission or task. Does NOT
               count toward the labor-pool minimums. Always
               carries a directing PC and a task text (see § 19).

Roles only apply to NPCs. Players are never counted toward
labor minimums or labor distribution — they're always PCs, not
community workers.


6. ROLE MINIMUMS (SRD § 08)
---------------------------
The NPC labor pool (NPCs whose role is NOT "Assigned") has
minimum fractions:

  GATHERERS     ≥ 33 %  (rounded down)
  MAINTAINERS   ≥ 20 %  (rounded down)
  SAFETY          5 – 10 %  (at least 1 NPC if the pool is tiny)

These directly affect the weekly Morale Check via the
"Enough Hands" slot (section 14):

  - All three met → "Enough Hands" contributes +1 to Morale.
  - For each group below its minimum → −1 (capped at −3).

The role bars in the community body show current counts vs.
minimums. Green bar = OK; red bar = understaffed. A small
orange tick on the dashboard bars marks the SRD minimum line.

The "⚖ Re-balance Roles" button auto-assigns NPCs to roles
based on their skills (Farming / Scavenging / Survival →
Gatherer; Mechanic / Tinkerer → Maintainer; Tactics / Ranged
Combat / Melee Combat / Heavy Weapons / Demolitions →
Safety). Use it to quickly top up understaffed groups.


7. THE LEADER
-------------
The Leader is one PC or NPC who rolls the weekly Morale Check
on behalf of the community. By default the founder is leader
until somebody is explicitly set.

Setting the leader: Inside the community body, the "Leader"
row has a dropdown listing every member. Picking one writes
either leader_user_id (for PCs) or leader_npc_id (for NPCs)
on the community row. Only one leader at a time.

Having no leader costs −1 on "A Clear Voice" every Morale
Check — so staying leaderless is usually a bad idea.


8. STEPPING DOWN AND LEADER SUCCESSION
--------------------------------------
The GM (always) and the current PC leader (themselves) can
abdicate via the "Step Down" button next to the Leader
dropdown.

A successor picker opens:

  - AUTO (default): picks the next Founder if any, otherwise
    the longest-tenured active member. The picker names who
    auto would choose so you can see the consequence.
  - LEAVE LEADERLESS: clears both leader fields. Next Morale
    Check applies the −1 A Clear Voice penalty.
  - A specific member: any other member of the community.

Auto-succession also fires when a Leader is REMOVED from the
community. If someone's role changes or they leave entirely
and they were the leader, the next successor is picked
automatically with no prompt.


9. RECRUITMENT CHECK
--------------------
Recruitment is the mechanical way PCs bring NPCs into a
Community during play. Open the Recruit modal via:

  * NPC card → "Recruit" button (during an active session).
  * Community ▾ → Recruit.
  * Checks ▾ → Recruit (while session is active).

The modal has three steps: pick (approach + skill) → roll →
result. Rolls use:

  2d6  +  AMod (roller's INF attribute)
       +  SMod (roller's level in the chosen skill)
       +  CMod  (First Impression + any other relevant CMods
                 including Insight Dice bonuses)

Standard XSE outcome ladder applies: 0–3 Dire Failure, 4–8
Failure, 9–13 Success, 14+ Wild Success. Matching dice trigger
Moment of Low Insight (1+1) and Moment of High Insight (6+6).

On success the NPC is added to the chosen Community with
recruitment type matching the chosen approach.


10. RECRUITMENT APPROACHES
--------------------------
COHORT — a shared interest or goal. NPC joins until the next
Morale Check, then re-evaluates.

CONSCRIPT — coerced service. Requires a credible threat;
see section 12 below.

CONVERT — shared belief, ideology, or vision. Joins as a
probationary Convert; commits fully after surviving the next
Morale Check.

(A fourth option — Apprentice — is unlocked only on a
Moment of High Insight; see section 11.)


11. APPRENTICES
---------------
If a Recruitment Check lands on a Moment of High Insight
(double-6), the rolling PC may claim the new NPC as their
Apprentice. Wild Success (plain 14+) alone does NOT unlock
the Apprentice option. One Apprentice per PC, ever.

Apprentice rules at creation (SRD § 08, CDP spends):
  - PC names the Apprentice (if they didn't have a name).
  - Roll 2d6 on both the Motivation and Complication tables
    (SRD Appendix A) and Fill In The Gaps on their background.
  - Spend 3 CDP on RAPID Range Attributes.
  - Pick one setting-appropriate Paradigm (SRD Table 8).
  - Spend 5 CDP on skills.
  - Over one month of game time, the PC can train the
    Apprentice in any single skill up to (PC skill level − 1).
  - Future CDP the PC earns can be spent on the Apprentice.

The Apprentice bond survives Morale-induced departures — they
stay bonded to their PC unless the PC dies, dismisses them,
or the Apprentice is killed.

In the community roster, Apprentice rows show a bond line:

    JESS BROOKER ⇐ ADA MAREN     [Unassigned ▾]  ×
      Task: Training with Ada in the garage        ✎ edit

Players can see their own Apprentice in the "You" row of the
At-a-Glance block (section 21). A dedicated "Apprentice" button
exists in the Community ▾ menu and on the character sheet as a
placeholder; the full Apprentice view comes in a future
release.


12. PRESSGANGING — THE CREDIBLE-THREAT RULE
-------------------------------------------
Conscription is coercion, not persuasion. Per SRD:

   "The PCs must present a credible threat for Conscription
    to work."

When you pick "Conscript" on the Recruit modal, a red warning
banner appears under the approach tiles reminding everyone
this is pressure. When you click Roll, a blocking confirm
dialog asks you to acknowledge: the PC has established a
credible threat (weapons drawn, leverage held, escape cut
off, etc.) before dice are thrown.

Cancelling the confirm dismisses the roll without burning any
Insight Dice or CMods. The other two approaches (Cohort,
Convert) never show this gate.


13. THE WEEKLY CHECK (FED → CLOTHED → MORALE)
---------------------------------------------
Once a week of in-game time, a Community must run the Weekly
Check. The GM clicks "Run Weekly Check" on the community body.
A single-button modal fires three rolls in sequence:

  1. FED CHECK — Gatherers roll for Rations.
     "Hunt / forage / farm / fish." Default assumption is
     that NPCs of reasonable proficiency roll (A = 0, S = 1);
     a PC can substitute their AMod / SMod if they're
     contributing.

  2. CLOTHED CHECK — Maintainers roll for Supplies.
     "Repair, scavenge, upkeep." Same default A/S as Fed.

  3. MORALE CHECK — the Leader rolls.
     2d6 + leader's INF AMod + leader's SMod in a chosen
     social skill + the six modifier slots + Additional
     (see section 14).

Fed and Clothed outcomes feed directly into the Morale
modifier slots — a great Fed adds +1 / +2 to Morale; a
famine drags it down by −2 or worse.

The leader is auto-detected from the community's leader
field; their INF attribute and social-skill levels auto-fill
the A/SMod inputs. The GM can override any number before
rolling.


14. THE SIX MORALE MODIFIER SLOTS + "ADDITIONAL"
------------------------------------------------
Each Weekly Morale Check has six built-in slot CMods plus
a GM-freeform slot:

  1. FED (post-roll) — set by this week's Fed Check outcome.
     +2 / +1 / 0 / −1 / −2 / −3 along the outcome ladder.

  2. CLOTHED (post-roll) — same ladder, driven by Clothed.

  3. MOOD AROUND THE CAMPFIRE — last week's Morale Check
     cmod_for_next carries over. Starts at 0 on week 1.

  4. ENOUGH HANDS — mechanical.
        +1 if all three role minimums are met.
        0 if one group is short.
       −1 per group below minimum (capped at −3).

  5. A CLEAR VOICE —  0 with an acknowledged leader,
                     −1 if leaderless.

  6. SOMEONE TO WATCH OVER ME —
        +1 if Safety is ≥ 10 % of the NPC labor pool.
         0 between 5 % and 10 %.
        −1 if Safety is below 5 %.

  7. ADDITIONAL (GM freeform) — Fill-In-The-Gaps for
     unmodeled events this week (raids, miracles, weather,
     a Distemper surge, a surprise resupply, etc.). Resets
     to 0 each time the modal opens.

Slots 3–6 auto-fill from community state; slots 1 and 2 snap
to the rolled Fed / Clothed outcomes. The GM can override
any slot via its small input.

Hover any slot label to read its SRD / CRB rule text in a
tooltip.


15. MORALE OUTCOMES
-------------------
    Roll         Outcome         Departures    Next Mood
    --------------------------------------------------------
    6+6          High Insight       none          +2
    14+          Wild Success       none          +1
    9–13         Success            none           0
    4–8          Failure            25 %          −1
    0–3          Dire Failure       50 %          −2
    1+1          Low Insight        75 %          −3

Departures soft-remove NPCs (not PCs — players control their
characters' actions). Within the removal pool, priority is:

    Unassigned first  →  Cohort  →  Convert  →  Conscript  →
    Founder  →  Apprentice (last).

So Apprentices and Founders stick with the community the
longest when times get hard.

Any success tier resets the community's consecutive-failure
counter to 0. Any failure tier increments it by 1.


16. DISSOLUTION ON THREE CONSECUTIVE FAILURES
---------------------------------------------
Three failed Morale Checks in a row collapses the community.
When you're at 2 / 3 the community panel shows a red warning
line: "one more failure dissolves the community."

If the third fail hits, the Result screen flips its
consequence panel to RED "⚠ Community Dissolves" and the
Finalize button relabels to "Finalize — Dissolve Community."
On confirm:
  - community.status → 'dissolved', dissolved_at = now.
  - Every active member is soft-removed with reason
    'dissolved'.
  - "Run Weekly Check" disappears from the community body.

Dissolved is permanent — there's no "re-form" path. A new
community must be created if you want a similar story.


17. THE RETENTION CHECK (SRD § 08 p. 22)
----------------------------------------
When the third failure would dissolve the community, a
"fast-acting leader" may attempt to salvage fragments via
an immediate Retention Check.

The dissolution panel offers a 🙏 Attempt Retention Check
button naming the current leader and their selected skill.
One click rolls a second Morale Check right there:

  2d6 + leader A/SMod + MOOD (= failed Morale's cmod_for_next)

Only Mood applies — no other slots. If the Retention Check
succeeds at any tier (Success / Wild Success / High Insight),
the community survives:

  - status stays active.
  - consecutive_failures drops to 2 (one week's cushion;
    another failure next week dissolves it).
  - Original failed-Morale departures still apply — people
    still left; but the community didn't collapse.

If the Retention Check fails (Failure / Dire / Low Insight),
dissolution proceeds as originally planned.

A Retention Check is a one-shot per failure — if the retention
roll itself fails, there's no second attempt.


18. SKIP WEEK (OFF-SCREEN TIME WITHOUT A ROLL)
----------------------------------------------
Sometimes a week passes in the fiction with nothing worth
resolving mechanically. The "Skip Week" button sits next to
"Run Weekly Check" on the community body. It:

  - Bumps week_number by 1.
  - Does NOT roll Fed / Clothed / Morale.
  - Does NOT change consecutive_failures, members, or Mood.

The next actual Weekly Check still carries Mood from the most
recent ROLLED check — skipped weeks don't reset it. This is
by design: narrative off-screen time shouldn't erase the
aftershocks of a real event.


19. ASSIGNED NPCs (PC-DIRECTED TASK LINKAGE)
--------------------------------------------
"Assigned" is a role for NPCs who are off doing a PC-directed
task. They don't count toward the labor-pool minimums —
they're out in the field, not at the homestead.

Setting an NPC's role to "Assigned" opens a small modal that
captures:
  - Directed by: which PC (campaign's character picker, with
    current community members listed first for convenience).
  - Task: a freeform text description. "Scouting the
    Belvedere's warehouse", "Running supplies to the mall."

Saving writes all three (role = assigned, assignment_pc_id,
current_task) in one round-trip. Cancelling leaves the role
on its old value — nothing commits.

Assigned rows render like Apprentice rows:

    QUINN LOCKHART #3 ⇐ ADA MAREN   [Assigned ▾]  ×
      Task: Scouting the warehouse              ✎ edit

Flipping the role OUT of "Assigned" (back to Gatherer,
Maintainer, Safety, or Unassigned) auto-clears both
assignment_pc_id and current_task. If the directing PC is
deleted from the campaign, the assignment clears via the
database FK but the NPC stays where they are — ready for
reassignment.


20. APPRENTICE TASK DELEGATION
------------------------------
Apprentice NPCs carry a "current_task" of their own. Anyone
with community access can see it; only the GM can edit for
now. (Master-PC edit is a natural extension and will land
when the Apprentice view is fully wired.)

On an Apprentice row with no task set, the GM sees a dashed
"+ Assign task" affordance. Click it, type the task, press
Enter or click Save. Task reads as italicized text below the
name line; a small ✎ edit reopens the input.

Empty-save clears the task back to idle.


21. THE AT-A-GLANCE BLOCK (PLAYER VIEW)
---------------------------------------
At the top of every expanded community body, a small blue
"At-a-Glance" block summarizes the community for the viewer:

  RECENT MORALE
     Five colored letter chips (W / H / S / F / D / L)
     showing the last five weekly outcomes, oldest left,
     newest right. Hover any chip to see the week number
     and full outcome name.

  YOU (when you're a member)
     Your role in the community (Gatherer / Maintainer /
     Safety / Unassigned / Assigned).
     "Founder" tag if that's your recruitment_type.
     Your Apprentice (if any) inline.
     A comma list of NPCs you have personally recruited
     into this community.

This block is visible to everyone — GMs see the same
summary. For players it's the primary view of the
community since the edit surfaces below are gated.


22. THE COMMUNITY DASHBOARD (GM VIEW)
-------------------------------------
Full-screen GM-only view at:

    /stories/<campaign-id>/community

Also reachable via Community ▾ → Dashboard.

Shows, per-community:
  - Header summary: status, week counter, consecutive
    failures, member count.
  - Morale History — last 20 weeks in a table with
    color-coded outcome badges, total rolled, member
    delta, and next-Mood CMod.
  - Resource History — last 40 Fed/Clothed rolls as
    compact color-coded chips grouped by week.
  - Current Role Distribution — horizontal bars vs. SRD
    minimum markers (orange tick on each bar).
  - Recruitment Stats — success rate overall and broken
    out by approach (Cohort / Conscript / Convert) plus
    Apprentice count.
  - Members by Recruitment Type — tiles showing how many
    Founders / Members / Cohorts / etc. you currently have.

Non-GMs who navigate here see an "access denied" screen
and a back-link to the campaign page.


23. ROLL LOG CARDS TO WATCH FOR
-------------------------------
In the session Logs tab and the Both tab, community events
render as dedicated color-coded cards:

  🤝 RECRUIT (Phase B)
     Outcome, approach, which community, whether Apprentice
     applied, Insight Dice spent.

  🌾 FED CHECK
     Week, community, dice, AMod/SMod/CMod, outcome, and
     the CMod that'll feed next Morale.

  🔧 CLOTHED CHECK
     Same shape as Fed.

  📊 MORALE CHECK
     Week, community, leader's name, which social skill
     they rolled, dice breakdown, slot breakdown ("Mood,
     Fed, Clothed, Hands, Voice, Watch, Additional"),
     outcome, departures by name (if any), consecutive
     failure counter, and the next-Mood CMod. Red border
     on the dissolve variant.

  🙏 RETENTION CHECK
     Leader, skill used, Mood (= failed Morale's next
     CMod), outcome. Green border if the community was
     saved; red if dissolution proceeded.


24. GLOSSARY
------------
AMod            Attribute Modifier. Value of the rolling
                character's RAPID attribute (Reason, Acumen,
                Physicality, Influence, Dexterity).

CMod            Circumstance Modifier. A numeric adjustment
                reflecting situational factors (First
                Impression, weather, leverage, community
                state, etc.).

Community       A Group of 13+ members running weekly
                mechanics.

Consecutive     Count of failed Morale Checks in a row.
Failures        Resets to 0 on any success. Hitting 3 triggers
                dissolution (possibly salvaged by a Retention
                Check).

Dire Failure    Roll total 0–3. 50 % of the community leaves.

Failure         Roll total 4–8. 25 % of the community leaves.

Fed Check       Weekly roll by Gatherers for Rations.

First           Per-PC / per-NPC CMod stored in
Impression      npc_relationships.relationship_cmod, set during
                a First Impression Check (SRD § 02).

Group           A PC party under 13 members. No weekly
                mechanics.

High Insight    6+6 on 2d6. Best outcome; Wild Success effects
                plus special perks depending on the roll type.

Insight Die     Pre-roll or post-roll bonus die; per-PC
                resource. Costs 1 Insight Die to spend.

Low Insight     1+1 on 2d6. Worst outcome; 75 % departures on
                Morale.

Mood Around     The cmod_for_next from last week's Morale
The Campfire    Check — carries forward as a slot on this
                week's Morale.

Morale Check    Weekly roll by the Leader to keep the community
                cohesive. 2d6 + leader A/SMod + 6 slot CMods +
                Additional.

Retention       Immediate salvage Morale Check when dissolution
Check           is triggered. Only Mood applies. One shot.

SMod            Skill Modifier. Level in the rolling
                character's relevant skill (0–4).

Success         Roll total 9–13. Morale holds.

Wild Success    Roll total 14+. Morale strengthens; +1 CMod
                on next Morale.
